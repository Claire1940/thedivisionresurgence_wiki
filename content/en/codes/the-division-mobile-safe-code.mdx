## Understanding Safe Mechanics in Resurgence

In *The Division Resurgence*, safes serve as environmental puzzles. Unlike standard loot crates that simply require an interaction, these locked boxes demand a four-digit or three-digit combination. The game generally provides the solution in one of two ways:

1.  **Environmental Clues:** Codes written on chalkboards, posters, or neon signs in the immediate vicinity of the safe.
2.  **Cryptic Clues (Collectibles):** Specialized intel items found near the safe that must be read in your "Database" menu under the "Cryptic Clues" tab.

> **💡 Tip:** If you find a safe but cannot see a code nearby, check your mini-map for a collectible icon. Picking up these "Cryptic Clue" items often reveals the combination through a riddle or a specific date mentioned in the text.

## Exploring the Streets: BoO Perimeter Safes

The area surrounding the Base of Operations is dense with loot. There are three specific safes located within walking distance of the main entrance.

### The Oaklock Store & Supermarket
Exit the Base of Operations and take an immediate right. Follow the street down until you see the Oaklock store on your left and a supermarket on your right.
1.  **Oaklock Store:** Enter the store and look behind the counter. The safe is on the floor. The code **122** is found on a decorative sign on the wall.
2.  **Supermarket:** Cross the street and enter the supermarket. Head to the back wall. The safe is tucked away, and the code **1987** is displayed on a sign directly above the unit.

### The Serotentia "Birthday" Safe
This safe is slightly more complex. From the BoO entrance, take a left and head down the street past the Madison Field Hospital. You will find a large safe inside a medical tent/building labeled Serotentia.
*   **The Code:** 1105
*   **The Clue:** You must first pick up a collectible on the ground outside the tent. This collectible is titled "Birthday Gift." Upon reading the entry in your Database, it mentions a date: November 5th. In the US format (MM/DD), this translates to 1105.

> **Warning:** Some players confuse the date format. If 1105 does not work for your specific regional version, try 0511, though 1105 is the standard for the global release.

## Safe House Caches

Safe houses are not just for restocking ammo; they often house hidden rewards for observant Agents.

### Autumn's Hope Safe House
Locate the NPC named Mullins at the reception desk. 
*   **The Code:** 1883
*   **The Clue:** Look at the "Established" date on the sign hanging behind the desk. It can be difficult to read depending on your graphics settings, but the year is 1883.

### Dante's Run Safe House
Exit the main safe room through the side door near the mannequin. This leads into an attached clothing store.
*   **The Code:** 7716
*   **The Clue:** Walk around to the front of the checkout desk. A promotional poster features the code in large print.

### Kerman Station Safe House
As you exit the main spawn room in Kerman Station, walk directly toward the locker area labeled "Safia Nerdies."
*   **The Code:** 7069
*   **The Clue:** A small yellow sticky note is pinned to the side of the lockers right next to the safe.

## FAQ

**Q: Does every safe in The Division Resurgence require a code?**

A: No. Most standard loot crates and weapon boxes can be opened with a simple interaction. Only the "Locked Safes" found in specific named locations require a manual code entry.

**Q: Can I share the division mobile safe code with my squadmates?**

A: Yes, the codes are universal for all players. If you are in a group, only one person needs to input the code for the safe to open, and everyone in the vicinity will receive their own individual loot drop.

**Q: I found a safe but the code isn't working. What should I do?**

A: Double-check that you are at the correct location. Many stores look similar in the NYC map. Also, ensure you haven't misread a "9" for an "8" or a "7" for a "1," as the environmental textures can sometimes be blurry on lower mobile settings.

**Q: Do safe rewards scale with my level?**

A: Yes. While the rarity (e.g., Superior) is often fixed, the actual stats and Gear Level of the items inside will scale based on your current character level and World Tier at the time of opening.
