## How to Solve The Division Resurgence Code Clue 08

The most sought-after puzzle for new Agents is the "Missing Daughter" safe. This specific objective is tied directly to the collectible entry for **the division resurgence code clue 08**. Unlike the simple three-digit locks found in storefronts, this safe requires you to engage with the game’s database system to find the solution.

### Location: The Cavern Safe House
To find this safe, you must head to **The Garments district**. Look for the player icon on your map that leads into the sewer system. Descend into the tunnels and follow the path until you reach **The Cavern safe house**. 

Inside The Cavern, you will find two points of interest:
1.  **The Safe:** Located near the central hub area.
2.  **The Artifact:** The physical "Code Clue 08 – Missing Daughter" document is found nearby on a desk.

### The Solution
Once you interact with the safe, ISAC will prompt you with a message: *"Please find ‘Code Clue 08’ in Database."* You must open your main menu, navigate to the **ISAC Database**, select **Collectibles**, then **Artifacts**, and finally **Cryptic Clues**. 

| Artifact Name | Clue Title | District | Safe Code |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Code Clue 08** | Missing Daughter | The Garments | **8122** |

By reading the description of the "Missing Daughter" document, you will find the four-digit sequence **8122**. Input this into the keypad to unlock the rewards, which typically include Superior (purple) gear and rare crafting components.

## Base of Operations Safe Locations

The Base of Operations (BoO) serves as your primary hub in New York, but many Agents run past the valuable loot hidden in its immediate vicinity. There are four major safes located in and around the BoO area. These are excellent for gathering materials early in your 2026 playthrough.

| Safe Location | Clue Type | Exact Code | Reward Tier |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **BoO Dormitories** | Notice Board | **1350** | Crafting Materials |
| **Oaklock Store** | Shop Sign | **122** | Gear Upgrade Parts |
| **Supermarket** | Wall Poster | **1987** | Consumables |
| **Serotentia (Tent)** | Birthday Gift | **1105** | Superior Gear |

### Navigating the BoO Area
To find the **Oaklock Store**, exit the BoO and take a right. The safe is on the floor, and the code **122** is written on a nearby promotional sign. Directly across the street is the **Supermarket**, where the code **1987** is displayed on a large sign above the refrigerated section.

The **Serotentia** safe is a bit more complex. Located to the left of the BoO entrance (near the old Madison Field Hospital site), you must first pick up a collectible on the ground. This "Birthday Gift" clue mentions "Tuesday 11th of May." In the game's four-digit logic, this translates to **1105**.

> **💡 Tip:** Always check your "Cryptic Clues" tab in the Database if a safe doesn't have a visible number nearby. Many four-digit codes are hidden within these lore entries.

## Safe House and Settlement Solutions

Beyond the main hub, various safe houses across the map contain hidden loot. These are often easier to miss because players use these locations primarily for fast travel and restocking.

### Spring Blossom Settlement
This is one of the first civilian hubs you will visit. To find the safe, you must climb to the upper residential tiers of the settlement.
*   **Location:** A room at the very top of the settlement.
*   **Clue:** A chalkboard to the right of the safe.
*   **Code:** **3580**

### Autumn's Hope Safe House
Upon arriving at Autumn's Hope, head to the reception area. Behind the NPC named Mullins, there is a sign indicating when the establishment was founded.
*   **Clue:** "Established [Year]" sign.
*   **Code:** **1883**

### Dante's Run Safe House
Exit the main spawn room and head toward the clothing store section of the safe house. Look for a mannequin; the safe is tucked behind the nearby service desk.
*   **Clue:** A poster on the front of the desk.
*   **Code:** **7716**

### Kerman Station Safe House
As you exit the fast travel room, look for the "Safia Nerdies" locker units. The code is on a small scrap of paper pinned to the side of the lockers.
*   **Clue:** Pinned note.
*   **Code:** **7069**

## Mastering the Safe Mechanic in 2026

Cracking safes in *The Division Resurgence* isn't just about knowing the numbers; it’s about understanding the environmental storytelling. Developers have designed these puzzles to encourage players to slow down and observe their surroundings.

### Types of Environmental Clues
*   **Chalkboards:** Common in civilian settlements like Spring Blossom. The numbers are usually written in large, clear strokes.
*   **Commercial Signs:** In shops like Oaklock or the Supermarket, the codes are often "sale prices" or "established dates."
*   **Cryptic Collectibles:** These are the most difficult. They appear as gold or blue glows on the ground. Once picked up, they must be "read" in the ISAC database to reveal the logic behind the code.

> **Warning:** Opening a safe does not pause the game. In "Dark Zone" adjacent areas or open-world sectors, ensure you have cleared all nearby hostiles before interacting with a keypad.

## FAQ

**Q: Where can I find the division resurgence code clue 08 in my inventory?**

A: It is not in your standard backpack. You must open the Main Menu, go to the **ISAC Database**, select **Collectibles**, then **Artifacts**, and look under the **Cryptic Clues** section.

**Q: Are safe codes different for every player?**

A: No, the codes are static. Whether you find the clue yourself or use a guide, the combination for a specific safe remains the same for everyone in the 2026 version of the game.

**Q: Can I loot the same safe multiple times?**

A: Generally, these are one-time rewards per character. Once a safe is cracked and the loot is claimed, it will remain open. Some major game updates or seasonal resets may occasionally refresh world loot, but this is rare.

**Q: What should I do if the code for Autumn's Hope (1883) isn't working?**

A: Ensure you are at the correct safe house. Some players confuse Autumn's Hope with Dante's Run. Also, double-check that you haven't accidentally misread an "8" for a "9" due to low graphical settings; the code is definitely **1883**.
