The biggest hurdle for most players is the mouse cursor. You need a way to lock the cursor so that moving your mouse moves the camera.
-   Right-click in the emulator's mapping tool to create an "Aim" point.

To sprint effectively, you cannot simply bind "W" to the joystick. You must use a "Slide" or "Swipe" mapping.
-   Create a slide action from the center of the movement joystick upward.

## Troubleshooting Common Emulator Issues

Even with a high-end PC, emulating mobile software can lead to unique bugs. If you experience "stuck" keys or camera spinning, it is likely a conflict between the emulator's overlay and the game's internal detection.

1.  **Camera Spinning:** This usually happens when the "Aim" point is placed too close to another UI element. Move the aim toggle icon to a "dead zone" on the screen where no buttons exist.
2.  **Low FPS despite high-end PC:** Check if your emulator is set to "60 FPS" in its own display settings. Many emulators cap at 30 FPS by default to save energy.
3.  **Input Lag:** Disable "Vertical Sync" (V-Sync) in both the emulator and the game settings. While this might cause slight screen tearing, it significantly reduces the delay between moving your mouse and seeing the camera move.
