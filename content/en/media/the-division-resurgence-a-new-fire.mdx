## The Origins of the Freemen Faction

The Freemen are not your typical group of looters. They are led by Arthur King, a former darling of Wall Street and a brilliant Silicon Valley inventor. King’s transition from a tech visionary to a radical insurgent is at the heart of the narrative in **the division resurgence a new fire**. Before the Dollar Flu struck, King had already grown disillusioned with the federal government, believing that city services were a facade for systemic control. When the pandemic hit, his worst fears were seemingly confirmed.

King’s radicalization reached its breaking point when his own family became victims of the crisis. Despite his resources and intellect, he could not save his wife from the illness, and his son died attempting to carry out his father’s increasingly erratic orders. This personal tragedy transformed King’s distrust into a "bitter hatred," as described by SHD intelligence. He now views the Division not as protectors, but as the "bad guys" responsible for the collapse of society and the loss of his family.

## Combat Mechanics: Facing the New Threat

Fighting the Freemen requires a different tactical approach compared to the Cleaners or the Rikers. Because their leader is a "maverick inventor," the Freemen utilize technology that mimics or counters SHD equipment. In **the division resurgence a new fire**, agents will encounter enemies who use specialized gadgets to disrupt skills and secure supply lines with ruthless efficiency.

The Freemen often prioritize securing supplies and medical resources, believing that the government is hoarding these items to maintain power. Their attacks are described as "intensifying all over the city," requiring agents to stay mobile and adapt to rapidly changing battlefield conditions.

### Key Enemy Archetypes

1.  **The Inventor-Scout**: Fast-moving units that deploy localized EMP jammers to disable player skills.
2.  **The Zealot**: Close-quarters combatants who use makeshift explosives, fueled by the belief that they are fighting for true freedom.
3.  **The Heavy Technician**: Armor-clad enemies who deploy automated turrets that can suppress an entire squad if not dealt with quickly.

> **Warning:** When engaging Freemen scouts, prioritize destroying their jamming devices immediately. If your skills stay offline for too long, their superior numbers will quickly overwhelm your position.

## Navigating the Intensifying Attacks

The Freemen are not content with staying in the shadows. Their presence in **the division resurgence a new fire** is marked by "intensifying attacks" across the open world. These encounters often trigger dynamic world events where players must protect supply drops or defend SHD outposts from waves of King’s followers.

### Tips for Open World Survival

*   **High Ground is King**: Most Freemen units lack long-range precision. Staying on rooftops or elevated platforms gives you a significant advantage.
*   **Target the Tech**: If you see a Freemen unit deploying a device, focus fire on the device first. Their tech is often more dangerous than their firearms.
*   **Squad Synergy**: Ensure at least one member of your squad is running a "Healer" or "Technician" build to counter the constant skill disruptions.

> **💡 Tip:** Use the "Pulse" skill frequently. The Freemen are experts at setting up ambushes in the shadows of the city's industrial zones.

## Arthur King’s Deranged Worldview

The narrative depth of **the division resurgence a new fire** explores the psychological toll of the Green Poison. Arthur King is a tragic figure, but his "deranged worldview" makes him one of the most dangerous antagonists the Division has ever faced. He doesn't just want to survive; he wants to dismantle the idea of the Division entirely. 

In his eyes, the Division agents are the ones prolonging the suffering of the people by upholding a "lie" of government stability. This ideological battle makes every encounter feel personal. The Freemen "rejected their lives" (the lives of ordinary citizens) to survive, and they view anyone still following the old rules as an enemy.

## FAQ

**Q: Who is the leader of the Freemen in The Division Resurgence?**

A: The Freemen are led by Arthur King, a former Wall Street and Silicon Valley inventor who turned against the government after the death of his wife and son during the pandemic.

**Q: How do I counter the EMP jammers used by the Freemen in the division resurgence a new fire?**

A: The best way to counter EMP jammers is to use gear with high Hazard Protection or to use "Mechanical" skills like turrets that can be placed before the jammer is activated. Additionally, prioritizing the destruction of the jamming device should be your first tactical objective.

**Q: Are the Freemen related to any other factions from the previous Division games?**

A: While they share some similarities with the LMB in terms of organization and the Cleaners in terms of zealotry, the Freemen are a unique faction specifically developed for *The Division Resurgence*, focusing on high-tech insurgency and anti-government ideology.

**Q: Can I join the Freemen faction in the game?**

A: No, players always take on the role of SHD (Strategic Homeland Division) agents. The Freemen serve as one of the primary antagonist groups you must defeat to progress through the story and secure the city.
