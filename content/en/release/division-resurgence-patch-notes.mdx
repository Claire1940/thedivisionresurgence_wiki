## New Game Modes: Survivors and Classified Assignments

The latest **division resurgence patch notes** confirm the addition of two major gameplay pillars designed to challenge even the most geared agents. The "Survivors" mode is a systemic reimagining of the classic survival experience from the original franchise, set against a transformed, snow-covered Washington D.C.

### Survivors Mode Features
In this mode, the environment is your primary adversary. Agents must manage:
*   **Body Temperature:** Seek heat sources or craft insulated clothing to survive the dynamic winter weather.
*   **Infection Levels:** Scavenge for antivirals to prevent the virus from overwhelming your system.
*   **Extraction Stakes:** Collect high-tier loot and call for a helicopter, but be prepared for "Hunter" ambushes.

### Classified Assignments
Returning from earlier entries in the series, these are bite-sized, story-driven missions located in unique, bespoke environments. These missions are puzzle-focused and provide deep lore collectibles regarding the origins of the Dollar Flu and the fall of the city.

## Faction Intel: The Rise of the Freemen

The world of Resurgence is occupied by four distinct enemy groups, each requiring a different tactical approach. The most dangerous addition in the 2026 cycle is the Freemen faction, led by the enigmatic Arthur King.

1.  **Raiders:** Low-threat criminals using Molotovs; easily dispatched with standard firearms.
2.  **Rikers:** Aggressive escaped convicts; watch for their flanking maneuvers and high-damage shotguns.
3.  **Cleaners:** Former construction workers obsessed with fire; aim for their oxygen tanks for explosive results.
4.  **Freemen:** Highly organized scientists and engineers. They utilize drones, turrets, and advanced technology that can disrupt your own SHD gadgets.

> **💡 Tip:** When engaging the Freemen, prioritize destroying their deployable tech first. Their drones can apply status effects that leave you vulnerable to their high-precision riflemen.

## Loot and Progression Changes

The "Rise Up" update introduces the Prototype Gear rarity. This is a long-term grind intended for endgame players who have reached level 30 expertise. Prototype items feature a new purple-hued rarity and stats that can reach up to 1.5 times the current maximums.

*   **Augments:** Every gear slot on a Prototype item now features a passive "Augment" perk.
*   **Recalibration:** While Prototype items are initially locked, the developers have confirmed that recalibration options will be added in a future post-launch patch.
*   **Dismantling:** Always pick up "junk" weapons. Dismantling these provides credits and materials essential for crafting high-end modifications at the base of operations.

## FAQ

**Q: Where can I find the official Division Resurgence patch notes?**

A: The full technical documentation is released on the official Ubisoft forums and the game's Discord channel immediately following the anniversary livestream. These notes detail specific bug fixes, weapon balance changes, and device-specific optimizations.

**Q: Is there cross-progression between mobile and PC?**

A: Yes, The Division Resurgence supports full cross-progression via your Ubisoft Connect account. You can start a mission on your phone and finish it on your PC without losing any progress.

**Q: How do I unlock the new Survivors mode?**

A: Survivors mode becomes available once you reach level 15 and complete the "Winter Transition" side quest in the main story campaign.

**Q: What is the best way to level up my agent quickly?**

A: While main missions provide the most XP, do not ignore side missions. They are much faster to complete and offer a higher XP-per-minute ratio, which is ideal for leveling up your agent during short play sessions.
