## Tactical Cover and Maneuvering

The golden rule for any SHD agent is to move from cover to cover. Standing in the open is a guaranteed way to end your mission prematurely. The tactical engine allows for fluid movement between obstacles, enabling you to outmaneuver and flank hostile forces.

> **Expert Tip:** Always anticipate the enemy's firing rhythm. Wait for the moment their current suppressive fire stops; this is your window to advance to a new position or return fire with precision.

If a hostile target manages to close the distance, do not panic. Use a melee strike to "whack" them hard, creating the space necessary to reposition or finish them off with a short-range firearm. Mastering the transition between defensive cover and aggressive flanking is a hallmark of a top-tier agent in any **division resurgence 攻略**.

## Advanced Field Tactics: Flanking and Melee

In the later stages of the game, enemies become significantly more aggressive. They will attempt to flush you out with their own grenades or rush your position with melee weapons. To counter this, you must maintain a high level of situational awareness.

*   **Flanking:** Use the environment to move around the enemy's line of sight. A flanked enemy loses their cover bonus and becomes vulnerable to rapid fire.
*   **Melee Defense:** When an enemy gets too close, use your melee bash to stagger them. This brief window of disorientation allows you to swap to a shotgun or SMG for a quick kill.
*   **Environmental Hazards:** Keep an eye out for explosive red canisters or leaking gas pipes that can be detonated to deal massive area damage.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the most important skill to master first in Division Resurgence?**

A: The most critical skill is cover-to-cover movement. Remaining stationary for too long makes you an easy target for snipers and grenades. Mastering the timing of when to move between cover is the foundation of any successful **division resurgence 攻略**.

**Q: How do I unlock my Signature Ability?**

A: Signature abilities are unlocked as you progress through the main story and reach specific level milestones. They represent the peak of SHD technology and require tactical energy to charge during combat.

**Q: Can I play the entire game solo?**

A: While much of the story content can be completed solo, the game is designed with social interaction in mind. High-level endgame content and Dark Zone incursions are much easier and more rewarding when tackled with a coordinated group of agents.

**Q: What should I do if I run out of ammunition during a long mission?**

A: Always keep a sidearm (pistol) equipped, as they have infinite reserve ammunition. Additionally, look for restock crates scattered throughout mission areas and loot fallen enemies to replenish your primary weapon magazines.
