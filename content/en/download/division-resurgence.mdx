## Getting Started: Servers and Settings

Your journey begins with a critical choice: selecting your regional server. Unlike many modern titles with fluid matchmaking, **division resurgence** utilizes isolated servers for North America, Europe, and Asia. This means that if you intend to play with friends or join a specific clan, every member must be on the same server from the start. Characters created on one server cannot currently interact with or transfer to another.

Once you have landed in the Base of Operations, your first priority should be technical optimization. The game defaults to a 30fps cap to preserve battery life, which can result in a "choppy" visual experience. If your device supports it, navigating to the settings menu to enable 45fps or 60fps is highly recommended. While this may slightly reduce certain graphical flourishes, the increase in combat responsiveness and smoothness is a significant tactical advantage.

## Choosing Your Specialization

In **division resurgence**, specializations function as your character class, defining your role within a squad. While you are prompted to pick one early on, the game is remarkably flexible, allowing you to switch between them at the Base of Operations. Each specialization comes with unique gadgets, a signature weapon, and a dedicated skill tree.

| Specialization | Role | Key Features | Signature Weapon |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Demolitionist** | DPS / AOE | Focuses on explosive damage and crowd control. | M32A1 Multi-Shot Grenade Launcher |
| **Field Medic** | Support / Healer | Deploys healing drones and poison-based offensive tools. | Reinforced Chem Launcher |
| **Bulwark** | Tank / Frontline | Uses heavy shields to draw aggro and protect allies. | Custom Shield & Sidearm |

> **💡 Tip:** Use the in-game Specialization Guide. It provides specific objectives that teach you the optimal "loops" for your chosen class and rewards you with essential upgrade points.

## Understanding Power Score and Gear Levels

A major departure from previous entries in the series is how gear functions. In **division resurgence**, your gear pieces have independent levels that are separate from your character level. This allows for a "forever gear" system where a high-end item found at level 10 can be continuously upgraded to remain viable even at level 40.

Your overall effectiveness is measured by **Power Score**. This metric determines your readiness for high-level content, such as Heroic Nests or Legendary Instances. Power Score is influenced by:
*   The base stats and rarity of your gear.
*   The level of your equipped weapon and gear mods.
*   Account-wide unlocks found in the "Achievements" and "Agent Career" menus.

| Gear Aspect | Impact on Agent | Upgrade Method |
|---|---|---|
| **Rarity** | Determines the number of attributes and talents. | Found via loot drops or crafting. |
| **Gear Level** | Scales the base armor and primary stats. | Upgraded using materials at the workbench. |
| **Protocols** | Provides specialized stat boosts (OS Protocols). | Requires specific upgrade materials. |
| **Mods** | Adds customizable stats like Crit Chance or Haste. | Slotted into gear pieces; can be leveled up. |

## Combat Mechanics: Damage Types

As you progress into the mid-to-late game of **division resurgence**, you will encounter enemies with specific elemental shields. Matching your weapon's damage type to these shields is mandatory for efficiency in higher difficulties. Weapons are categorized by colored icons:

1.  **Red (Blast):** Highly effective against armored targets and groups.
2.  **Light Blue (Shredding):** Designed to strip away standard enemy protections quickly.
3.  **Dark Blue (Piercing):** Ideal for bypassing heavy physical damage resistance.

It is advisable to keep at least one weapon of each damage type in your inventory at all times. Swapping weapons to match the "shield color" of a boss can be the difference between a successful extraction and a wipe.

## Maximizing Daily Progression

To maintain a healthy player ecosystem, **division resurgence** employs several daily limits and bonuses. Understanding these is key to efficient leveling without burning out.

*   **Daily XP Boost:** Look for the orange arrow next to your level bar. While orange, you gain 100% XP. Once it turns gray, your XP gains are significantly throttled for the remainder of the day.
*   **Open World Activity Limit:** You can receive full rewards from 10 open-world activities per day. After this, the loot quality drops significantly.
*   **Nests:** These are instanced combat encounters. Keep an eye out for "Heroic Nests," which rotate every 24 hours and offer the best farming opportunities for high-end materials.

> **Warning:** Side missions do not scale with your level in this game. If you receive a side mission at level 15, its XP reward is fixed. It is often better to complete these as soon as you get them to ensure the XP remains relevant to your progression.

## Exploration and Secret Loot

The streets of Manhattan are filled with more than just hostile factions. Exploration is rewarded through various environmental puzzles and collectibles.

### Lockbox Combinations
Occasionally, you will find high-value loot boxes that require a 3-digit code. These codes are never random. They are typically found in one of two ways:
1.  **Visual Clues:** Scanned on nearby walls, whiteboards, or computer screens within the same room as the box.
2.  **Database Collectibles:** Found in the "Cryptic Clues" tab of your database menu. If you pick up a collectible near a locked box, read it immediately; the code is likely hidden in the text.

### The Agent Career Track
If you feel overwhelmed by the number of icons on the map, focus on the **Agent Career** menu. This acts as a guided journey through the game's systems. Completing career milestones provides massive injections of XP, Phoenix Credits, and specialization points that are difficult to obtain elsewhere.

## Crafting and Economy Tips

The economy in **division resurgence** is tight, and materials are often more valuable than credits. When dealing with unneeded gear, the general rule is to **dismantle** rather than sell.

*   **Dismantling Returns:** When you dismantle an item you have previously upgraded, you receive roughly 90% of the invested materials back. For OS Protocols, this return drops to 80%.
*   **The Crafting Timer:** Unlike the instant crafting in previous games, this title uses a time-based system. Even basic items can take hours to craft, with high-end pieces taking up to 24 hours. Plan your crafting sessions before you log off for the day.
*   **The Workbench:** Avoid over-investing in crafting during the early levels (1-20). The RNG nature of the workbench makes it a "resource sink" until you have leveled it up sufficiently to guarantee higher-tier results.

## Monetization and the Store

As a free-to-play title, **division resurgence** features various microtransactions. While the game is entirely playable without spending money, certain purchases offer better value for those looking to support the developers:

1.  **Classified Ops Pass (Battle Pass):** Offers the highest value-to-cost ratio, providing an exotic weapon (The Warlord) early on and a steady stream of upgrade materials.
2.  **Warden Field Supplies:** A monthly subscription that grants daily Phoenix Credits, increased inventory slots, and a 40% reduction in crafting times.
3.  **Avoid Sealed Caches:** These are loot boxes with randomized odds. Unless you are looking for a specific cosmetic, your credits are better spent on guaranteed progression items.

> **Important:** Purchases in this game are character-bound, not account-bound. Ensure you are on your primary character before claiming any premium rewards or making store purchases.

## FAQ

**Q: Is Division Resurgence cross-play between iOS and Android?**

A: Yes, the game supports full cross-play between mobile platforms and even PC emulators. However, remember that players are locked to their specific regional server (NA, EU, or Asia).

**Q: Can I play the entire campaign solo?**

A: Most of the main story and open-world activities can be completed solo. However, Legendary Instances and high-tier Dark Zone extractions are significantly easier and more rewarding when played in a squad of four.

**Q: How do I unlock the Dark Zone?**

A: The Dark Zone unlocks naturally as you progress through the main campaign, typically around level 12-15. It remains the primary source for "Contaminated Loot," which must be extracted via helicopter to be used.

**Q: What happens if I reach the daily XP cap?**

A: You can continue to play, but your experience gains will be reduced by roughly 70-80%. This is a good time to focus on cleaning up your inventory, managing your clan, or exploring the map for collectibles that do not rely on XP gains.
