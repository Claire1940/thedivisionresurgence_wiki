For players looking to dive into the narrative, exploring **the division resurgence campaign missions** provides the primary path for leveling up and unlocking essential gear. ## Key The Division Resurgence Campaign Missions Breakdown

The early stages of the campaign serve as a high-stakes introduction to the game's mechanics and the central conflict in Manhattan. Unlike previous entries, Resurgence introduces a more personal look at the civilian struggle through the lens of the Spring Blossom settlement.

### The Spring Blossom Escort
Your first major assignment involves the extraction of Angela Chan and her nephew, Kevin, from the Spring Blossom Restaurant. This mission introduces the "Escort" mechanic, where players must clear pathing for civilians while defending against localized skirmishes. The JTF has lost control of several checkpoints in Midtown, meaning you will face hostile resistance almost immediately upon leaving the restaurant.

| Mission Phase | Key Objective | Primary Threat |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Restaurant Extraction** | Meet Angela Chan and secure the perimeter. | Initial Rioter waves |
| **Path Clearing** | Neutralize gunners at the Midtown checkpoints. | Snipers and flanking units |
| **Bridge Checkpoint** | Reach the Manhattan Bridge for JTF extraction. | Heavy reinforcements |

### The Trinity Church Field Hospital
Following the initial escort, the narrative shifts to the Trinity Church, where Doc O'Neill is managing a makeshift field hospital. This mission highlights the escalating threat level as enemies begin utilizing heavy weaponry, including .50 caliber machine guns and RPGs. Players are tasked with defending the hospital from an all-out assault led by a hostile leader named Jax.

> **💡 Tip:** When defending the church interior, prioritize the RPG gunners on the upper balconies. Their splash damage can quickly deplete your armor and endanger the hostages.

## Showdown at Liberty Island

The climax of the prologue takes place at Liberty Island. After Jax takes Doc O'Neill and several nurses hostage, you must pursue them via boat to the iconic landmark. This mission is critical for progressing through **the division resurgence campaign missions** as it introduces complex objective-based combat, including bomb defusal.

Jax rigs the ferry and the surrounding area with explosives. While your partner covers the perimeter, you must interact with the EOD (Explosive Ordnance Disposal) prompts to save the hostages. The mission concludes with a boss fight against Jax, who utilizes high-mobility tactics and heavy cover. Successful completion of this arc triggers a significant narrative jump, placing your agent in a coma for over a month and re-introducing you to the world as a veteran of the "First Wave."

## Factions and Combat Intel

As you continue progressing through **the division resurgence campaign missions**, the variety of enemies increases. By the time you wake up in the medical wing under the supervision of Acting Commander Faye Lau, the landscape of Manhattan has changed.

| Faction | Combat Style | Notable Leaders |

The "Free Men" are a new addition to the lore in 2026, often targeting civilian stockpiles. They are known for their technical ingenuity, often using modified industrial equipment to create makeshift turrets and shields.

## Settlement Management: Spring Blossom

A core component of **the division resurgence campaign missions** is the development of the Spring Blossom Settlement. Located in a secure area of Midtown, this hub serves as your base of operations for crafting, social interaction, and mission briefing.

*   **Angela Chan:** Focuses on the humanitarian aspect, advocating for a "Beacon of Hope" without excessive visible weaponry.
*   **Kevin:** The security lead who pushes for automated defenses, searchlights, and anti-personnel mines.
*   **Doc O'Neill:** Runs the clinic and provides medical-themed side quests to recover untouched caches of supplies.

To maximize the rewards for **the division resurgence campaign missions**, you must balance these perspectives. Upgrading the settlement's infrastructure—such as the power grid and fresh water systems—unlocks higher-tier vendors and better gear blueprints.

## Tactics for High-Difficulty Missions

When tackling the later stages of the campaign, standard cover-to-cover movement may not be enough. The difficulty of **the division resurgence campaign missions** scales with your World Tier, requiring more synergy between your skills and gear.

1.  **Coordinate Elemental Effects:** Use incendiary or EMP grenades to disrupt the Free Men's technical equipment.
2.  **Focus on the Medic:** In missions involving the LMB remnants, always eliminate the combat medics first to prevent enemy revives.
3.  **Utilize the Environment:** Many mission areas include explosive barrels or hanging loads that can be dropped on enemies to save ammunition.
4.  **Upgrade Your Signature Weapon:** Ensure your specialized kit is leveled up before entering boss-heavy missions like the assault on the Clinton stronghold.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I play the division resurgence campaign missions solo?**

A: Yes, the entire story campaign is designed to be playable solo. However, the difficulty scales based on the number of players in your group, and bringing friends can make some of the "Horde" style defense objectives much easier to manage.

**Q: How do I unlock the Second Wave missions?**

A: The Second Wave storyline becomes available after you complete the Liberty Island arc and the subsequent "Coma" transition. You will be briefed by Faye Lau at the Base of Operations to begin these new chapters.

**Q: What happens if a hostage dies during a mission?**

A: Most campaign missions have "fail states" for critical NPCs. If a hostage like Doc O'Neill is killed, the mission will typically restart from the last checkpoint. Ensure you draw aggro away from non-combatants during these sequences.

**Q: Are there missable rewards in the early campaign?**

A: Most rewards are tied to mission completion and cannot be "missed." However, exploring side rooms during missions can lead to finding "Echoes" and audio logs that provide additional XP and lore context for the state of NYC in 2026.
