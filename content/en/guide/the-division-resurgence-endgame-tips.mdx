## The Foundation of Endgame: Crafting Expertise

In *The Division Resurgence*, the crafting bench is not just a tool for filling gear gaps; it is a primary progression system. Unlike the leveling phase where you constantly swap items, the endgame focuses on the **Crafting Expertise** level. Every time you craft an item, you gain experience in this category.

Even if you do not currently need a specific piece of gear, you should be crafting daily. At lower expertise levels, your chances of rolling high-quality gear are slim. However, as you reach Expertise Level 10, the rewards become significantly more lucrative.

### Crafting Expertise Probability Table

| Expertise Level | Green (Standard) Chance | Blue (Specialized) Chance | Superior Chance | High-End Chance |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Level 1** | 70% | 30% | 0% | 0% |
| **Level 5** | 20% | 50% | 30% | 0% |
| **Level 10** | 0% | 0% | 75% | **25%** |

> **💡 Tip:** Crafting a single item currently takes 24 hours. Because you are limited by time, you must keep the queue full at all times to reach Expertise Level 10 as quickly as possible.

## Mastering Blueprints and Tactical Augmentations

To craft the best gear, you must first secure the blueprints. In *The Division Resurgence*, blueprints are automatically unlocked the moment you find a new item in the world—whether from a boss drop, a mission reward, or a loot crate. Note that this rule does not apply to Exotics; those must be found through specific high-tier activities.

Once a blueprint is unlocked, you can enhance it using **Tactical Augmentations**. These are rare resources found in the open world or through endgame challenges. Investing these into a specific blueprint (like the Vector SMG or a specific Mask) provides permanent stat boosts to every version of that item you craft in the future.

## Weapon Proficiency: The Path to Mastery

Beyond the stats on the page, your personal "familiarity" with a weapon type matters. The **Proficiency System** rewards you for sticking with your favorite tools. By using a weapon to neutralize enemies (provided they are not more than five levels lower than you), you earn proficiency points. 

Each proficiency level grants specific stat enhancements to that weapon blueprint, such as improved accuracy, horizontal stability, or vertical recoil control. Reaching Proficiency Level 10 on your primary weapon is a core goal for any endgame build.

## Equipment Improvement and Recalibration

Once you reach Level 20, you unlock the **Equipment Improvement Station** at the Base of Operations. This is where you fine-tune your "God Rolls." There are two main ways to enhance your gear here:

### 1. The "Improve" Function
As your character level increases, your gear can fall behind. Use the "Improve" function to spend resources and bump the power level of your current items to match your agent's level. This ensures your favorite High-End weapon remains viable as you progress through higher World Tiers.

### 2. Recalibration Rules
Recalibration allows you to swap out a stat or talent that doesn't fit your build. However, there is a catch: you can only recalibrate **one** slot per item. Once you change a talent or an attribute, all other slots on that item are locked permanently. 

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not waste recalibration resources on "Superior" (purple) gear. Save these rare materials for High-End (gold) or Exotic (red) items, as these are the only tiers that truly matter in the 2026 endgame.

### Potential Recalibration Attributes

| Attribute Type | Possible Rolls | Optimal For |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Offensive** | Crit Hit Damage, Armor Piercing, Multi-shot Chance | DPS Builds |
| **Defensive** | Total Armor, Health Regen, Protection from Elites | Tank/Frontline |
| **Utility** | Skill Intensity, Skill Duration, Cooldown Reduction | Medic/Tech Builds |

## High-Tier Endgame Activities

Once your Power Score is sufficiently high, you should focus your time on activities that offer the highest "Time vs. Reward" ratio. The 2026 endgame meta revolves around three primary pillars:

1.  **The Dark Zone (DZ):** This is the most efficient way to farm High-End gear. While the threat of Rogue agents is real, the "Landmarks" in the DZ provide guaranteed boss spawns with high drop rates for classified items.
2.  **Resistance (Horde Mode):** A test of endurance where you fight waves of enemies. The "Reward Crate" inside Resistance maps has a high probability of dropping Exotics if you can survive long enough to afford the resource cost.
3.  **Legendary Missions:** These are specialized versions of campaign missions with highly intelligent AI. Completing these weekly grants a guaranteed Exotic Cache and a massive amount of Phoenix Credits.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the fastest way to increase my Power Score in the endgame?**

A: The fastest way is to consistently upgrade your World Tier. As soon as you hit the gear score requirement for the next tier, move up. This scales the loot drops to a higher base power level, allowing you to replace your older gear with stronger variants. Additionally, use the "Improve" function at the equipment station to keep your best weapons at your current character level.

**Q: Can I recalibrate Exotic weapons in The Division Resurgence?**

A: Yes, you can recalibrate Exotics at the Equipment Improvement Station starting at Level 20. However, the resource cost is significantly higher than High-End gear. Remember that you can only change one attribute or talent, so choose the one that least benefits your current build.

**Q: Why should I bother with the crafting queue if I already have good loot?**

A: Crafting is the only way to reliably increase your Crafting Expertise. Even if you don't need the items you are making, reaching Expertise Level 10 gives you a 25% chance to craft High-End gear from any blueprint. These **the division resurgence endgame tips** emphasize that expertise is a long-term investment that pays off when you start hunting for perfect gear rolls.

**Q: How do I unlock more blueprints for endgame crafting?**

A: Blueprints are unlocked by simply acquiring the item. Farm World Tier 5 missions, open field caches, and clear Dark Zone landmarks. Every new weapon or gear piece you pick up (excluding Exotics) will permanently add its blueprint to your crafting bench.
