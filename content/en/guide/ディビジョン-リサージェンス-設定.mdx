## Comprehensive Key Configuration and Layout

The interface for a mobile cover-based shooter is naturally complex. To succeed, you must arrange your buttons to allow for simultaneous movement, aiming, and ability usage. Most top-tier players move away from the default layout to a "claw" grip or a customized three-finger setup.

The following table outlines the default command structure which you can modify within the settings menu:

| Action | Default Command / Location | Tactical Importance |
|---|---|---|
| **Movement** | Left Side Slide (Hold to Sprint) | Essential for repositioning and flanking. |
| **Hip Fire / Lean** | Left Side Fire Button | High priority for close-quarters combat. |
| **Main Shoot** | Right Side Bullet Icon | Primary interaction; hold for ADS fire. |
| **ADS (Aim Down Sights)** | Icon above Right Fire Button | Critical for long-range precision. |
| **Cover Entry** | Left of Right Fire Button | The core mechanic for damage mitigation. |
| **Weapon Swap** | Bottom Center Icon | Quick transitions between primary/secondary. |
| **Abilities/Skills** | Right of Weapon Swap | Use for drones, turrets, and shields. |
| **Reload / Dodge** | Bottom Left/Right of Fire | Vital for uptime and avoiding grenades. |

> **💡 Tip:** Increasing the size of the "Cover" and "Dodge" buttons can prevent accidental deaths during high-intensity firefights where finger precision might slip.

## Mastering the Cover Mode Settings

One of the most unique aspects of the **ディビジョン リサージェンス 設定** is how the game handles environmental interaction. Unlike the PC version where a dedicated key is always used, mobile players can choose between automation and manual control. Selecting the right mode depends on your comfort level with touch controls.

| Mode | Functionality | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **Manual** | Player must tap the button to enter/exit cover. | Advanced players who want total control. |
| **Auto** | Automatically sticks to cover when near it. | Beginners who want to focus purely on aiming. |
| **Custom** | Hybrid settings based on specific triggers. | Players looking for a tailored balance. |

Manual mode is generally recommended for competitive play. It prevents "sticky" situations where your character might accidentally lock onto a wall when you actually intended to sprint past it. However, if you find the screen too cluttered, the Auto mode simplifies the movement loop significantly.

## Optimized ADS and Sensitivity Settings

In 2026, the meta for mobile shooters emphasizes high visibility. A common mistake players make in their **ディビジョン リサージェンス 設定** is leaving the ADS (Aim Down Sights) view on the default first-person perspective for all weapons. 

For most engagement ranges, switching your ADS view to **Third-Person** is superior. This allows you to maintain a wider field of view (FOV), making it easier to spot flankers while you are zoomed in. Even with Marksman Rifles, the third-person accuracy in Resurgence is remarkably high.

### Recommended Sensitivity Scaling

While sensitivity is subjective, these baseline values provide a stable starting point for most modern smartphones:

1. **Standard Camera Sensitivity:** 60-70% (Allows for quick 180-degree turns).
2. **ADS Sensitivity:** 40-50% (Provides enough friction for precise headshots).
3. **Scope Sensitivity (4x/8x):** 25-35% (Slow enough to track moving targets at distance).
4. **Vertical Sensitivity Ratio:** 0.8 (Slightly lower than horizontal to help with recoil control).

## Graphics and Performance Optimization

To maintain a competitive edge, your frame rate must remain stable. Even on high-end devices in 2026, thermal throttling can occur during long sessions in the Dark Zone. Adjusting your visual **ディビジョン リサージェンス 設定** is mandatory for consistent performance.

*   **Frame Rate:** Always set this to "Max" or "60/120 FPS" regardless of other settings. Smoothness is more important than texture quality.
*   **Shadow Quality:** Medium or Low. High-quality shadows consume significant GPU resources with minimal tactical benefit.
*   **Particle Effects:** Low. In a game with many explosions and chemical clouds, high particle counts can cause frame drops during the most critical moments.
*   **Post-Processing:** Off. This reduces visual blur and makes enemies stand out more clearly against the environment.

> **⚠️ Warning:** High graphics settings will drain your battery rapidly. If playing away from a charger, consider a "Power Saving" profile that prioritizes frame rate over resolution.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I use a controller with my ディビジョン リサージェンス 設定?**

A: Yes, the game supports most Bluetooth controllers. When a controller is connected, the key config menu will update to allow for button remapping specific to your gamepad.

**Q: What is the best "Cover Mode" for PvP?**

A: Most competitive players prefer "Manual" cover. This prevents the character from accidentally sticking to objects while trying to "strafe" or "dance" during a gunfight, which is a common tactic in the Dark Zone.

**Q: How do I fix lag or frame drops in the middle of a mission?**

A: First, check your thermal settings. If your phone is hot, lower your "Resolution" and "Graphics Quality" in the settings menu. Also, ensure that "Background App Refresh" is turned off on your device to dedicate all resources to the game.

**Q: Is there a way to auto-loot items?**

A: You can find auto-loot options within the "Gameplay" tab of the settings. You can filter this to only pick up items of a certain rarity (e.g., Blue/Superior or higher) to avoid filling your inventory with junk.
