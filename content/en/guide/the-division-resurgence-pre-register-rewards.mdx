## How to Claim Your Rewards

Earning these items is only the first step; you must manually claim them to see them appear in your in-game mailbox. Unlike some mobile games that automatically grant items, Ubisoft requires a specific redemption path through their social platform.

1.  **Link Your Accounts**: Ensure both The Division 2 (on console/PC) and The Division Resurgence (on mobile) are signed into the exact same Ubisoft Connect account.
2.  **Complete the Tutorial**: You must finish the initial "Grand Central Terminal" prologue in Resurgence before the rewards section becomes accessible.
3.  **Visit Ubisoft Connect**: Open the Ubisoft Connect overlay within the game menu or visit the official [Ubisoft Connect website](https://ubisoftconnect.com/).
4.  **Navigate to Rewards**: Look for the "Resurgence" tab under your games list.
5.  **Manual Redemption**: Click "Claim" on each individual item. Once claimed, they will be sent to your in-game "Deliveries" or "Mailbox" tab.

## Monetization and The Battle Pass

As a free-to-play title, Resurgence features several monetization layers. However, the developers have emphasized that the core story and tactical gameplay remain accessible without spending money. **The division resurgence pre register rewards** help mitigate some of the early-game grind, but here is what the long-term economy looks like:

*   **Battle Pass**: Features both a free and premium track. The premium track typically includes exclusive weapon skins and faster material acquisition.
*   **Cosmetic Events**: Time-limited "Apparel Caches" that allow players to pull for high-end outfits.
*   **Field Supply Subscription**: A monthly service that provides extra inventory slots and reduced crafting timers—perfect for dedicated daily players.
*   **Booster Packs**: One-time purchases that offer a bundle of crafting materials to speed up gear progression.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Be cautious when purchasing "Caches." While they offer great gear, the RNG (randomness) means you aren't guaranteed a specific item unless it is part of a direct-purchase bundle.
