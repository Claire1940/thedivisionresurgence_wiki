## Core Components of The Division Resurgence Demolition Build

The foundation of any successful Demolitionist lies in the Engineering stat. Unlike builds that prioritize armor or health, a high-performing explosive build requires you to lean entirely into skill intensity and multi-shot chances. Because you will be playing from the backline, your primary goal is to eliminate threats before they can close the gap.

### Key Stat Priority

When rolling or upgrading your gear, you should follow a strict hierarchy of attributes. Since the Demolitionist relies on skills rather than sustained weapon fire, traditional weapon damage is secondary to skill-based metrics.

| Priority | Stat Name | Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Primary** | **Engineering** | Directly increases the base damage of all explosive skills. |
| **Secondary** | **Skill Multi-shot Chance** | Grants a chance for skills to trigger additional hits or explosions. |
| **Tertiary** | **Skill Intensity** | Improves the effectiveness and scaling of skill-based status effects. |
| **Utility** | **Skill Radius** | Expands the area of effect for grenades and turrets. |

> **💡 Tip:** Do not worry about Armor or Health stats for this specific build. If you are positioned correctly, you should rarely take direct fire, making survivability stats a wasted slot in a pure DPS configuration.

## Recommended Weapons and Talents

For **the division resurgence demolition build**, your weapon serves more as a "stat stick" than a primary source of damage. You want weapons that offer high Engineering rolls and talents that trigger bonuses for your skills.

The **Warlord** assault rifle is a popular choice for this build, particularly if you can roll it with Engineering as the primary attribute. Look for weapons that offer "Skill Piercing Damage" and "Skill Multi-shot Chance" as secondary attributes.

### Best Weapon Talents

1. **Engineering Mastery**: Any talent that provides a flat percentage increase to your Engineering stat should be prioritized.
2. **Skill Intensity Boost**: Look for rolls that increase intensity after a skill kill, creating a snowball effect during combat encounters.
3. **Multi-shot Synergy**: Talents that reward critical hits with increased skill cooldown reduction can help keep your explosives active more frequently.

## Gear Sets and Synergy

To maximize **the division resurgence demolition build** DPS, you must utilize set bonuses that align with skill radius and signature ability charge. The current 2026 meta favors a hybrid approach between two primary sets: **Fire Cycle** and **Jackpot**.

### The 4-Piece Fire Cycle Meta

The Fire Cycle set is generally found in Tier 2 content and is widely considered the "gold standard" for explosive builds. Its bonuses significantly increase your skill radius, ensuring that your grenades and turrets cover the widest possible area.

| Set Pieces | Bonus Granted | Why it Matters |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **2 Pieces** | +Skill Radius | Makes it harder for enemies to dodge your explosive circles. |
| **4 Pieces** | +Multi-shot Chance / Signature Charge | Increases the frequency of high-damage procs and special abilities. |
| **2 Pieces (Jackpot)** | +Skill Multi-shot Chance | Provides a flat boost to your overall explosive damage output. |

### Essential Gear Talents

Your chest piece and backpack are the most important slots for unique talents. For a long-range Demolitionist, the following are highly recommended:

*   **Toy Maker**: This talent provides a 21% skill multi-shot chance bonus as long as you remain at least 20 meters away from your target. This perfectly complements the backline playstyle.
*   **Glass Cannon**: If you are confident in your positioning, Glass Cannon can increase your total damage by up to 28%, though it significantly increases the damage you receive.
*   **Intense Performance**: This talent increases your skill radius further, which is vital for ensuring your turrets and grenades hit multiple targets in dense mobs.

## Protocols and Skill Mods

Protocols are often overlooked but provide the "fine-tuning" needed for high-level play. For this build, the **Brains over Brawn** protocol is a mandatory inclusion.

### Protocol Optimization

The **Brains over Brawn** protocol converts a percentage of your total Engineering stat into additional skill damage. At higher upgrade levels, it can also grant over 20% Skill Critical Chance. To obtain these, you should consistently run [The Division Resurgence official site](https://www.ubisoft.com/en-us/game/the-division/resurgence) events and weekly bounties, focusing on the main targets to farm purple or blue protocol drops.

### Skill Mod Selection

You should aim for a three-piece **Applied Demolitions** set for your skill mods. This set specifically buffs the damage and recharge rate of your explosive skills. 

1.  **Frag Grenade Mods**: Focus on radius and damage.
2.  **Artillery Turret Mods**: Focus on cooldown reduction and multi-shot chance.
3.  **Seeker Mine Mods**: Focus on target tracking and explosion yield.

> **Warning:** Avoid mixing too many different mod types. Sticking to a 3-piece set bonus is much more effective than using several unrelated high-tier mods.

## Farming and Progression Strategy

Building the ultimate **the division resurgence demolition build** requires a dedicated farming route. Because gear mods and protocols are RNG-dependent, efficiency is key.

### Gear Mod Farming: Hostile Nests
A highly effective method for leveling your gear mods to level 20 is the **Hostile Nest reset**. You do not need to wait for the 15-minute timer mentioned in the game UI. By standing outside the nest and re-entering immediately after completion, you can farm the sub-rewards repeatedly. This is the fastest way to acquire the materials needed to max out your Engineering mods.

### Protocol Farming: Weekly Bounties
Protocols are best farmed through the Weekly Bounty system. By repeating the main target mission, you can accumulate a high volume of blue and purple protocols. Even if you don't get the "perfect" roll immediately, you can upgrade blue protocols fully in a single day of dedicated farming to bridge the gap until you find legendary versions.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I use the division resurgence demolition build for solo play?**

A: Yes, but you must be extremely careful with your positioning. Since this is a "glass cannon" build, you should use your turret as a distraction and stay at least 20 meters away to keep the Toy Maker talent active.

**Q: Is Fire Cycle better than the Jackpot set?**

A: For a pure Demolitionist, a 4-piece Fire Cycle set is generally superior due to the skill radius and signature charge bonuses. However, a 2-piece Jackpot splash is recommended to pick up the extra multi-shot chance.

**Q: How do I increase my Engineering stat quickly?**

A: The fastest way is to equip Engineering-specific gear mods and level them to 20 using materials gathered from Hostile Nests. Additionally, look for "Engineering" as the main stat on all your armor pieces.

**Q: What is the best secondary skill for this build?**

A: While the Artillery Turret is the primary choice, the Seeker Mine or the Chem Launcher (Oxidizer) work well as secondary skills to provide additional area denial and explosive damage.
