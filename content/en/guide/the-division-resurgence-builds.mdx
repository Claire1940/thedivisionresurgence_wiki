### Targeted Loot and Gear Mods
In the Dark Zone, you can choose which set bonuses you want to target. This is essential for fine-tuning your stats. For example, if you are running a DPS build, you may want to target mods that provide "Rapid Charge" or "Steady Hands."

*   **DZ Credits:** You can farm up to 10,000 credits weekly. These are used at the Base of Operations vendor.
*   **Deconstruction:** Breaking down unwanted DZ mods provides a small amount of currency that does not count toward your weekly cap.
*   **Vendor Resets:** The DZ vendor refreshes daily, often offering T1 guns and exotic recalibration books.

## Advanced Recalibration and Enhancement

Once you have secured your base gear, the real work begins with recalibration. Every exotic weapon has a corresponding "Recalibration Book" that can be found by defeating bosses in the Dark Zone. These books allow you to re-roll weapon talents to better suit your specific **the division resurgence builds**.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not buy recalibration books from the vendor unless you are desperate. They drop frequently from DZ bosses, and your credits are better spent on Exotic Weapon Enhancement Kits.

### Enhancement Kits
Exotic Weapon Enhancement Kits are rare items used to upgrade the tier of your weapon. These are often limited in quantity, so it is vital to only use them on weapons with "God Roll" talents (e.g., Steady Hands on a Lady Death).

## Dark Zone Survival and Extraction Strategies

To finalize your **the division resurgence builds**, you must successfully extract your contaminated loot. The mobile version introduces several new mechanics to help solo players and groups alike.

1.  **Sentry Turrets:** You can deploy automated turrets at extraction zones for a small credit fee. These will defend you against AI waves and rogue agents.
2.  **The Smuggler:** Found in safe houses, the Smuggler can sneak out up to three items for 300 DZ credits. This is a "safe" way to secure high-value gear if the extraction zones are being camped by rogues.
3.  **Infection Levels:** Pay attention to the contamination meter. If it reaches 100%, you will die instantly. Use the safe house terminals to reset your infection level between clearing landmarks.
4.  **Second Rope Extraction:** After the initial extraction timer, a second rope appears. This allows you to exit the Dark Zone entirely without having to run back to a checkpoint door.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the best way to farm for the division resurgence builds?**

A: The most efficient way to farm is by participating in Dark Zone sessions and selecting "Targeted Loot" for mods. This allows you to specifically hunt for the set bonuses that complement your specialization. Additionally, clearing heroic landmarks provides the highest chance for exotic drops.

**Q: Can I change my specialization after I've started a build?**

A: Yes, you can switch specializations at the Base of Operations. However, keep in mind that you will need to level up each specialization's skill tree independently to unlock its full potential for your agent.

**Q: How do Dark Zone credits work in 2026?**

A: You can earn up to 10,000 credits per week through gameplay. You can also gain extra credits by deconstructing mods, which does not count toward your weekly limit. Use these credits to buy exotic augments and vanity items from the DZ vendor.

**Q: Is it better to play solo or in a group for gear farming?**

A: While solo play is possible, heroic bosses and landmarks are balanced for four-man groups. Grouping up increases your clear speed and provides safety during the extraction process, especially when rogue agents are active in your bracket.
