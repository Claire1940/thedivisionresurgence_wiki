## Overview of Specializations in 2026

In Division Resurgence, specializations act as the game's class system. Unlike the mainline console games where you can swap builds freely later in the game, the mobile version places a heavier emphasis on your chosen "Specialty." Each class comes with a fixed Signature Weapon and a set of combat skills that cannot be used by other roles.

> **⚠️ Warning:** You choose your first specialty during the tutorial. You cannot change your class until you reach Level 12 or progress significantly through the main story. Choose carefully based on your preferred playstyle!

### Core Class Mechanics
Each specialty consists of three main components:
1.  **Signature Weapon:** A high-power weapon with limited ammo that can turn the tide of a boss fight.
2.  **Active Combat Skills:** Two unique skills (like turrets or shields) specific to that class.
3.  **Passive Talents:** Buffs that improve weapon handling, armor recovery, or skill haste.

## Detailed Analysis of Top Specialties

### 1. Demolitionist: The King of AOE
The Demolitionist remains at the top of the **ディビジョン リサージェンス 専門 職 おすすめ** list for players who want pure destructive power. This class excels at clearing groups of enemies and forcing NPCs out of cover, which is a frequent challenge in the mobile environment.

*   **Signature Weapon:** The M32A1 Grenade Launcher deals massive explosive damage in a wide radius. It is perfect for destroying enemy armor or clearing out a room of "Purifiers" instantly.
*   **Key Skills:**
    *   **Artillery Turret:** Deals sustained explosive damage from a distance.
    *   **Seeker Mine:** Automatically tracks enemies and explodes, making it easy to use on touchscreens.
*   **Why choose it?** It is the most beginner-friendly class. You don't need precise aiming for explosives, making it ideal for mobile play.

### 2. Bulwark: The Unstoppable Force
If you prefer to be in the face of the enemy, the Bulwark is your best bet. This class is designed to soak up damage and protect teammates, making it essential for high-level raids and difficult missions.

*   **Signature Weapon:** The Breach Combo allows you to use a specialized shield alongside powerful close-quarters attacks.
*   **Key Skills:**
    *   **Phalanx Shield:** A portable cover that lets you move and shoot simultaneously.
    *   **Shockwave Spike:** A crowd-control tool that stuns enemies, leaving them vulnerable to your squad.
*   **Why choose it?** It provides the highest survivability in the game. In solo play, it allows you to survive mistakes that would kill other classes.

### 3. Field Medic: The Essential Support
No squad is complete without a Field Medic. In the 2026 meta, sustain is everything. While everyone has access to basic medkits, the Field Medic has infinite healing potential through their skills.

*   **Signature Weapon:** A specialized launcher that fires healing or chemical clouds.
*   **Key Skills:**
    *   **Healing Hive:** Automatically repairs the armor of any allies within its radius.
    *   **Reinforcer Chem Launcher:** Fires a canister that creates a cloud of armor-repairing nanobots.
*   **Why choose it?** Medics are always in high demand for Co-op play. Additionally, they can deal decent damage over time using chemical clouds, making them surprisingly viable for solo players who play defensively.

## How to Unlock and Change Specialties

As mentioned earlier, your initial choice is locked for the first few hours of gameplay. However, Division Resurgence is designed to let you eventually master all roles.

1.  **Reach Level 12:** This is the standard threshold where the game introduces the ability to swap between unlocked specializations.
2.  **Complete Specialization Quests:** To unlock a *new* specialty (one you didn't pick at the start), you must complete specific mission chains or "Field Research" tasks.
3.  **Visit the SHD Hub:** Once unlocked, you can change your specialty at any time while in a safe zone or the main social hub.
4.  **Upgrade Your Tree:** Each specialty has its own talent tree. You earn "Specialization Points" by leveling up while that class is active. These points are used to increase weapon damage, skill duration, and signature ammo drop rates.

For more official updates on class balance, check the [official Division Resurgence website](https://www.ubisoft.com/en-us/game/the-division/resurgence) for the latest patch notes.

## Tactical Tips for 2026

*   **Ammo Management:** Signature weapon ammo is rare. Save your grenades or specialized bolts for "Named Elites" (yellow health bars) rather than wasting them on red-bar enemies.
*   **Skill Synergies:** If you are playing in a group, try to coordinate. A **Vanguard** using Pulse to highlight enemies combined with a **Demolitionist** firing into the highlighted area is a devastating combo.
*   **Cover is Key:** Even with the Bulwark's shield, you cannot stand in the open forever. Always look for "blue" cover indicators on your UI to ensure you are receiving the maximum defensive bonuses.
*   **Don't Ignore Sidearms:** Since Signature weapons have limited ammo, ensure your sidearm is upgraded. It is your fallback when things go wrong.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the absolute best specialty for a total beginner?**

A: The **Demolitionist** is widely considered the best starting point for the **ディビジョン リサージェンス 専門 職 おすすめ** rankings. Its skills are "set and forget," and the grenade launcher is very forgiving if your aim isn't perfect on a mobile screen.

**Q: Can I play as a Field Medic and still do damage?**

A: Yes. While the Medic's primary focus is healing, their chemical launcher skills can be modified to fire corrosive gas that deals significant damage over time to enemies trapped in cover.

**Q: How do I get more Signature Weapon ammo?**

A: Signature ammo drops from powerful enemies or through specific kills (like headshots or explosive kills) depending on your specialty's talent tree. You can also find ammo crates in safe houses and before boss rooms.

**Q: When can I finally change my specialty if I don't like my first choice?**

A: You will need to reach Level 12 and complete the introductory story arc in Manhattan. After that, the "Specialization" menu will unlock in your character screen, allowing you to begin the research for other classes.
