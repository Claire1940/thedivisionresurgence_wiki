## Detailed Safe Walkthroughs

### Spring Blossom Settlement
The first safe most Agents encounter is located within the Spring Blossom Settlement. After fast-traveling to the area, move toward the upper levels of the settlement buildings. You will find a small room containing a standard black floor safe. 

To find the code yourself, look at the chalkboard positioned just to the right of the safe. The numbers **3580** are clearly written there. Inputting this code typically yields a "Superior" (Purple) item, making it a mandatory stop for new players looking to replace their starting gear.

### Base of Operations (BoO) Dormitories
Even your home base contains hidden loot. Once you have access to the NYC Base of Operations, head toward the dormitory wing. Before you reach the safe, keep an eye on the notice boards lining the hallway. A small piece of paper pinned to the board displays the numbers **1350**. The safe is located just a few steps further down the corridor.

### The Oak Lock & Supermarket Safes
Just outside the main entrance of the Base of Operations, there is a street containing two distinct loot opportunities. 
1.  **Oak Lock Store:** Take a right from the BoO entrance and enter the Oak Lock shop on the left side of the street. The safe is on the floor, and the code **122** is found on the store's promotional signage.
2.  **Supermarket:** Directly across the street from the Oak Lock store is a supermarket. Head to the back wall. You will see a sign high up on the wall with the year **1987**. Use this to open the safe on the floor beneath it.

## Solving Cryptic Clues: The Serotentia Safe

Unlike the previous examples, some safes require you to pick up a specific collectible to trigger the "clue" logic in your menu. Near the Madison Field Hospital (a landmark familiar to veterans of the original game), you will find the Serotentia building. 

Outside the entrance, there is a collectible on the ground. Picking it up reveals the clue: "Birthday Gift." The note mentions a birthday on Tuesday, the 11th of May (or 5th of November depending on regional formatting). In the context of the game's logic, the **division resurgence safe code** for this large vault is **1105**. This safe is particularly valuable as it often contains rare crafting components alongside gear.

## Safe House Secrets

Safe houses serve as your primary respawn and social hubs, but they are also home to hidden caches that many players run right past.

### Autumn's Hope
Inside the Autumn's Hope safe house, look near the NPC named Mullins at the reception desk. There is a sign behind the desk that lists the "Established" year of the building. While the graphics can be slightly blurry depending on your mobile settings, the year is **1883**. This is the combination for the safe sitting right next to the desk.

### Dante's Run
At Dante's Run, exit the main spawn room and head toward the attached clothing store (look for the mannequins). Behind the main checkout counter is a safe. The code **7716** can be found on a bright poster on the front of the desk or the adjacent wall.

### Kerman Station
Finally, at Kerman Station, navigate toward the locker area near the exit. You will see a set of "Safia Nerdies" lockers. A small yellow note is pinned to the side of the lockers with the code **7069**.

## Exploration Strategy for Agents

To find every **division resurgence safe code** in 2026, you should adopt a methodical approach to clearing zones. When you enter a new district:

## FAQ

**Q: Can I open a safe more than once?**

A: No, safes in The Division Resurgence are generally one-time rewards per character. Once you input the correct code and claim the loot, the safe will remain open and cannot be looted again.

**Q: What should I do if the division resurgence safe code isn't working?**

A: Double-check the numbers. Some codes, like the one in Autumn's Hope (1883), can be hard to read due to texture resolution. Also, ensure you have picked up any required "Clue" collectibles, as some safes may remain uninteractable until the clue is in your inventory.

**Q: Are the codes the same for every player?**

A: Yes, these combinations are static and do not change between players or sessions. You can use the codes listed in this guide immediately without finding the in-game clue first.

**Q: Do these safes contain premium currency?**

A: Generally, no. These safes provide gameplay rewards such as weapons, armor, and crafting materials. Premium currency is typically handled through mission rewards, battle passes, or the in-game store.
