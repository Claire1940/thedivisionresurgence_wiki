## Weapon Categories and Archetypes

The diversity of **the division resurgence weapons** allows players to specialize in various combat roles. Each category has its own inherent strengths, such as the critical hit chance of SMGs or the armor-shredding capabilities of LMGs.

### Assault Rifles and SMGs
Assault Rifles (ARs) like the **M416** and **ACR** remain the most versatile tools for mid-range engagements. In 2026, the **StG44** and **Type 81** have also emerged as strong contenders for players who prefer higher per-bullet damage. SMGs, including the **Vector 45 ACP** and **MP7**, dominate close-quarters combat by rewarding high-mobility playstyles.

### Rifles and Marksman Rifles
For players who value precision, Rifles like the **M1A** and the **Urban MDR** offer high semi-auto damage. If you prefer long-range sniping, Marksman Rifles (MMRs) such as the **M700 Carbon** and **SVD** provide the highest burst damage in the game, often capable of one-shotting elite enemies when aimed at headshot weak points.

### LMGs and Shotguns
LMGs like the **M60** and **MG5** are essential for suppression and sustained fire against tanky bosses. Conversely, Shotguns like the **ACS-12** and **SPAS-12** are the ultimate "get off me" tools, providing massive stagger and burst damage at point-blank range.

## Crafting and Blueprint Mechanics

Success in the endgame relies heavily on the crafting station located at the Base of Operations in New York. To craft any of **the division resurgence weapons**, you must first unlock its specific blueprint. Blueprints are generally acquired automatically the first time you loot a specific weapon in the world, though Exotic blueprints are a notable exception and cannot be crafted through standard means.

### Crafting Expertise Levels
Every time you craft an item, you gain Crafting Expertise. Increasing this level is vital for long-term progression because it directly influences the quality of the items you produce.

| Expertise Level | Superior (Purple) Chance | High-End (Gold) Chance |
|---|---|---|
| **Level 1** | 30% | 0% |
| **Level 5** | 55% | 5% |
| **Level 10** | **75%** | **25%** |

Items typically take 24 hours to craft, so it is recommended to keep your crafting queue full at all times to maximize your expertise gains.

## Weapon Proficiency and Enhancements

Even after you have acquired a high-end firearm, the journey to perfection continues through the Proficiency and Tactical Augmentation systems. These systems allow you to "level up" specific weapon blueprints, making every future version of that gun you craft significantly stronger.

1.  **Weapon Proficiency:** By using a weapon (e.g., the Vector) to neutralize enemies that are not more than 5 levels lower than you, you earn Proficiency points.
2.  **Stat Buffs:** Reaching Proficiency Level 10 grants permanent bonuses to accuracy, vertical stability, and horizontal stability for that specific weapon model.
3.  **Tactical Augmentations:** These are rare resources found in the open world. Investing them into a blueprint can provide massive flat damage increases, sometimes reaching up to an 80% damage bonus at maximum investment.

## Recalibration: Fine-Tuning Your Build

Once you reach Level 20, you unlock the Equipment Improvement Station. This allows you to perform Recalibration on High-End and Exotic gear. Recalibration is the process of replacing an undesirable stat or talent with a better one.

> **⚠️ Warning:** You can only select **one** modifier on a weapon to recalibrate. Once you change that attribute or talent, all other slots on that weapon are locked and cannot be modified. However, you can re-roll that specific slot as many times as you want if you have the resources.

### Recalibration Priority
When looking at your **the division resurgence weapons**, prioritize re-rolling "dead" stats like Received Healing or Skill Duration (on a non-skill build) into high-value offensive stats:
*   Weapon Damage %
*   Critical Hit Damage
*   Armor Piercing
*   Headshot Damage

For more information on the latest seasonal updates and weapon balance changes, check the [official The Division Resurgence website](https://www.ubisoft.com/en-us/game/the-division/resurgence).

## FAQ

**Q: How do I unlock new weapon blueprints in The Division Resurgence?**

A: Most blueprints for **the division resurgence weapons** are unlocked automatically the first time you loot that specific weapon from a mission reward, an enemy drop, or a container in the open world. Exotic weapons do not currently have craftable blueprints.

**Q: What is the maximum level for weapon proficiency?**

A: You can level up your proficiency with any specific weapon model up to Level 10. Each level provides incremental bonuses to handling stats like stability and accuracy, which apply to all future versions of that weapon you craft or find.

**Q: Can I recalibrate Superior (purple) rarity weapons?**

A: No. Recalibration is reserved exclusively for High-End (gold) and Exotic quality equipment. If you have a Superior weapon with poor rolls, it is better to deconstruct it for materials or use it to increase your Crafting Expertise.

**Q: Why does my crafted weapon have different stats than the preview?**

A: The crafting menu shows a range of possible attributes and power scores. The exact numbers and talents are randomized upon completion of the 24-hour crafting timer. You can improve your odds of getting better stats by increasing your Crafting Expertise level.
