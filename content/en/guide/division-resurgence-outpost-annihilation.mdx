## Navigating the Dark Zone Map and Landmarks

The Dark Zone in Division Resurgence is a familiar yet evolved landscape, mirroring much of the iconic layout from the original Manhattan crisis. Landmarks serve as the primary hubs for combat activity, where players face waves of enemies to secure high-tier loot. Unlike standard missions, these areas feature dynamic difficulty scaling based on your current level and the specific bracket you occupy.

| Activity Type | Description | Respawn Timer |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Landmarks** | Wave-based combat zones featuring purple and gold bar enemies. | 3 Minutes |
| **Contaminated Areas** | High-intensity zones that require infection level management. | Constant |
| **Heroic Bosses** | Single, extremely tough "Enemy Leaders" located in the map center. | 10 Minutes |
| **Extraction Points** | Designated zones where loot must be airlifted out via helicopter. | Variable |

> **💡 Tip:** Always restock your ammo at safe houses or crates before entering a landmark. The Dark Zone does not automatically refill your reserves, and running dry mid-fight is a death sentence.

## Mastering Targeted Loot and Currency Caps

One of the most significant features of the Resurgence Dark Zone is the ability to influence your drops. While you cannot target specific weapons directly, you can set "Targeted Loot" for gear mods. This allows you to focus on two-set or three-set piece bonuses that complement your specific specialization.

However, your efficiency is governed by weekly limits. Understanding these caps is vital for long-term progression:

| Currency/Item | Weekly Cap | Source |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Dark Zone Credits** | **10,000** | Completing sessions, looting world boxes. |
| **Dark Zone Intel** | **20,000** | Killing bosses and clearing landmarks. |
| **Deconstruction Credits** | **No Cap** | Breaking down unwanted mods for small credit returns. |

The Dark Zone vendor, located in the Base of Operations, offers a rotating stock of T1 weapons, exotic enhancement kits, and recalibration books (such as those for the Lady Death). These stocks refresh daily, so check in regularly to see if a god-roll talent has appeared.

## Survival Tactics: Infection and Rogues

When entering contaminated areas, a new UI element appears next to your health bar: the infection percentage. If this meter reaches 100%, your agent will perish instantly. To prevent this, you must visit Safe Houses to reset your infection level.

Safe Houses also offer unique "Smuggling" services. If the extraction points are being camped by Rogue agents, you can pay a fee of **300 DZ Credits** to have a smuggler safely remove three items from the zone for you. This bypassed the need for a helicopter but is limited in capacity.

### Combatting Rogue Agents
If you choose to go Rogue or need to hunt those who have, the Dark Zone provides tools for both sides:
*   **SHD Terminals:** Use these to hack the system and reset your Rogue status.
*   **Position Tracking:** Spend credits at a Safe House to reveal the locations of all other agents on the map for 30 seconds.
*   **Loot Randomization:** Note that if a Rogue kills you, the loot they pick up from your body is randomized. This prevents "real-money trading" and ensures the economy remains balanced.

## Extraction Strategies and Outpost Annihilation

The climax of any Dark Zone run is the extraction. In Division Resurgence, the process has a unique twist designed to assist solo players. When you call for a helicopter, you must defend the area for one minute against spawning NPC enemies.

To increase your chances of success, look for **Sentry Turrets** near the extraction zone. For a small credit fee, you can activate these automated defenses to provide cover fire while you wait for the rope. 

> **⚠️ Warning:** Once the first rope is loaded, a **second rope** will appear shortly after. This second rope allows you to extract yourself directly from the Dark Zone without having to run back to a physical exit gate.

## Dark Zone Feats and Commendations

As you spend time in the zone, you will earn "Feats" based on your performance. These act similarly to the "Survival" mechanics in previous titles, rewarding you with better loot and higher credit payouts upon exit.

*   **Survivalist:** Earned for staying alive for extended periods.
*   **Salvage:** Earned for looting hidden crates throughout the map.
*   **Reaper:** Earned for high kill counts against elite factions.
*   **Neutralizer:** Earned for taking down Rogue agents or Bosses.

## FAQ

**Q: What happens if I die during a division resurgence outpost annihilation attempt?**

A: If you die in the Dark Zone, your contaminated loot drops to the floor. You can respawn and attempt to track back to your "Last Death" location to recover it, but other players (Rogues) may get there first.

**Q: Is the Lady Death exotic available through Dark Zone vendors?**

A: Yes, the Dark Zone vendor occasionally sells the Lady Death and its associated recalibration books. However, it is highly recommended to farm bosses for these items first to save your limited Dark Zone credits.

**Q: Can I play the Dark Zone solo in 2026?**

A: While solo play is possible, the Dark Zone—especially heroic bosses—is balanced for four-man groups. Solo players should utilize the Smuggler and Sentry Turret mechanics to compensate for the lack of teammates.

**Q: Does the infection level persist after I leave the Dark Zone?**

A: No, your infection level resets once you exit the Dark Zone or die. However, you must actively manage it while inside contaminated areas by visiting safe houses to avoid instant death.
