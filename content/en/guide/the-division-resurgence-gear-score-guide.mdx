## Power Score vs. Gear Level: Key Differences

In previous Division titles, your Gear Score was a direct reflection of the items you had equipped. In *The Division Resurgence*, the system has evolved. You now manage two primary metrics: **Gear Level** and **Power Score**.

Your Gear Level is independent of your character level. This means a high-end backpack found at level 10 can be upgraded and used all the way to level 40 if the stats and talents remain relevant to your build. However, your Power Score is the "gatekeeper" for content. If a mission recommends a Power Score of 15,000 and you are only at 12,000, you will face significant damage penalties regardless of your character level.

| Metric | Scope | How to Increase |
|---|---|---|
| **Gear Level** | Individual Item | Using upgrade materials to boost base stats. |
| **Power Score** | Total Account Strength | Equipping higher-tier gear, leveling mods, and earning achievements. |
| **Character Level** | Player Progression | Gaining XP through missions, nests, and open-world activities. |

> **💡 Tip:** Do not feel pressured to constantly swap gear for higher-level drops. If you find a High-End item with a perfect talent, keep it and use the "Improve" function to level it up alongside your character.

## Gear Rarity and Tiered Progression

As you progress toward the 2026 endgame, you will notice that gear is not just categorized by color (Rarity), but also by Tier. While High-End (Gold) gear is generally superior to Superior (Purple) gear, the introduction of Tier 1 (T1) and Tier 2 (T2) items adds another layer of depth.

| Gear Rarity | Color | Significance |
|---|---|---|
| **Standard** | Green | Basic starter gear; dismantle immediately for materials. |
| **Specialized** | Blue | Early-game filler; provides basic stat boosts. |
| **Superior** | Purple | Reliable mid-game gear; T1 versions appear in late-game. |
| **High-End** | Gold | The standard for builds; features unique talents and T2 variants. |
| **Exotic** | Red/Orange | Unique items with game-changing talents (e.g., The Warlord). |

### Understanding Tiers (T0, T1, T2)
Items found during the initial campaign are considered Tier 0. As you reach the level 30-40 bracket, you will begin finding Tier 1 and eventually Tier 2 gear. A T1 Purple item may actually provide better raw stats than a T0 Gold item, though it will lack the powerful High-End talent. Always prioritize T2 High-End gear for your final level 40 build.

## The Role of Mods and OS Protocols

Your Power Score is heavily influenced by "hidden" gear slots. Every piece of equipment has mod slots that can be filled with offensive, defensive, or utility chips. These mods themselves have levels and can be upgraded using specific materials found in Hostile Nests.

### OS Protocols: The Watch Talent
The OS Protocol (located in your SHD Watch slot) is perhaps the most critical component of **the division resurgence gear score guide** for endgame players. These protocols act as a passive "super talent." 
*   **Firepower Protocols:** Massive boosts to weapon damage and crit.
*   **Tactician Protocols:** Enhances skill haste and skill power.
*   **Survivor Protocols:** Focuses on armor regeneration and health.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Be extremely careful when dismantling OS Protocols. While standard gear returns 90% of invested materials, OS Protocols only return 80%. This 20% loss can be devastating if you frequently swap between different protocol types.

## Weapon Damage Types and Elemental Shields

In the 2026 meta, simply having high DPS is not enough. Enemies in higher-tier content utilize specific shields that are resistant to standard gunfire. You must match your weapon's damage type to the enemy's shield color to effectively "shred" their defenses.

| Damage Type | Icon Color | Best Used Against |
|---|---|---|
| **Blast** | Red | Heavy armor and explosive-resistant units. |
| **Shredding** | Light Blue | Standard kinetic energy shields. |
| **Piercing** | Dark Blue | High-density tech shields and robotic enemies. |

To maintain a high Power Score efficiency, ensure your loadout includes at least two different damage types. This prevents you from being "hard-countered" by a boss with a specific elemental resistance.

## Efficient Farming: Daily and Weekly Loops

To keep your gear score competitive, you must engage with the game's daily progression caps. *The Division Resurgence* utilizes a "Daily XP Boost" (indicated by an orange arrow near your level). Once this boost is exhausted, your XP gains drop significantly.

1.  **Daily Quests:** Complete these first for Phoenix Credits and specialization XP.
2.  **Open World Activities:** You can receive rewards from exactly 10 activities per day. Focus on "Hostage Rescues" or "Supply Runs" depending on which upgrade materials you need.
3.  **Hostile Nests:** These rotate every 15 minutes. They are the primary source for gear and weapon mod upgrade materials.
4.  **The Dark Zone:** If playing in a group, the Dark Zone offers the highest density of High-End T1 and T2 drops.

## Crafting and Recalibration Strategy

Crafting in Resurgence is a time-gated process. Even common items can take up to 24 hours to complete unless you have specific boosters like the Warden Field Supplies. 

*   **Don't Craft Early:** Most gear found in the open world will outclass crafted items during the leveling process.
*   **Save Materials:** Hoard your "Electronics Omega Components" and "Recall Booklets." You will need hundreds of these at level 40 to "God Roll" your T2 High-End gear.
*   **Recalibration:** Use Recall Booklets to swap out a poor stat for a better one. Only do this on High-End gear that already has at least two "Best in Slot" attributes.

| Material Type | Primary Source | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| **Gear Parts** | Dismantling Armor | Upgrading Gear Level. |
| **Weapon Parts** | Dismantling Guns | Upgrading Weapon Level. |
| **Omega Components** | Weekly Bounties | High-level Specialization upgrades. |
| **Recall Booklets** | Store / Weekly Quests | Recalibrating gear stats. |

## FAQ

**Q: What is the fastest way to increase my Power Score?**

A: The most immediate way to boost Power Score is to upgrade your gear mods and OS Protocol. While finding better armor helps, the cumulative bonuses from high-level mods often provide a larger boost than a single piece of gear.

**Q: Should I sell or dismantle my old gear?**

A: In 2026, dismantling is almost always the better option. Credits are easy to come by through missions, but the materials returned (90% of investment) are vital for keeping your current gear competitive.

**Q: Is the Gear Score system different from The Division 2?**

A: Yes. In *The Division Resurgence*, gear has its own level that you can manually increase. In *The Division 2*, gear drops at a fixed score based on the world tier. This makes individual "God Rolled" items much more valuable in Resurgence since you can keep them forever.

**Q: How do I unlock Tier 2 gear?**

A: Tier 2 gear is locked behind endgame milestones. You typically begin seeing T2 drops after reaching character level 40 and completing specific Heroic-level Nest challenges or high-intensity Dark Zone landmarks. Following this **the division resurgence gear score guide** will help you reach those milestones faster.
