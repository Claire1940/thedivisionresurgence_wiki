## The Armory System: Unlocking Your Potential

The foundation of every powerful build in The Division Resurgence begins in the Armory. Accessed via the Phoenix icon in the top-right corner of your HUD, the Armory serves as the central hub for all weapon and gear blueprints. You cannot simply find every weapon in the wild; many of the best tools of the trade must be unlocked and refined through consistent play.

To unlock new blueprints, you must spend Proficiency Points. These points are earned naturally through almost every activity in the game, from completing main story missions to participating in open-world encounters.

| Blueprint Type | Starting Proficiency Cost | Key Examples |

> **💡 Tip:** Don't hoard your Proficiency Points early on. Spend them to unlock the base versions of weapons you enjoy using, as this allows you to start building "Crafting Expertise" immediately.

## Tactical Augmentation: The New Way to Mod

In The Division Resurgence, the concept of "modding" extends beyond just physical attachments like scopes and silencers. The "Tactical Augmentation" system allows you to apply permanent upgrades to a weapon's blueprint. When you acquire Tactical Augmentation resources—often dropped by difficult bosses—you can slot them into a specific weapon's blueprint to provide massive stat boosts.

### How Augmentation Slots Work
Every blueprint has a "Tactical Augmentation" tab. By filling these slots, you ensure that every future version of that weapon you craft will be significantly stronger.

*   **Slot 1:** Typically increases bonus attributes (e.g., Critical Hit Damage) by 30%.
*   **Slot 2:** Can increase those same attributes by a staggering 60%.
*   **Talent Slots:** Advanced augmentations can even increase the base level of the talents that roll on the weapon.

It is important to remember that these "mods" are tied to the blueprint itself. If you craft a weapon and *then* upgrade the blueprint's augmentation, the weapon in your inventory will not automatically update. You will need to craft a new version to benefit from the improved stats.

## Crafting Expertise and Rarity Probabilities

Once a blueprint is unlocked, you must increase its "Crafting Expertise." This represents your character's familiarity with that specific weapon model. By spending Proficiency Points to level up a blueprint's expertise, you increase the probability of crafting higher-rarity versions of that item.

| Expertise Level | Common Chance | Rare Chance | Epic Chance | High-End Chance |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Level 1** | 70% | 25% | 5% | 0% |
| **Level 6** | 38% | 38% | 19% | 5% |
| **Level 7** | 36% | 36% | 20% | 8% |
| **Level 15+** | < 20% | < 20% | 40% | 20%+ |

At Expertise Level 15, you unlock **Crafting Focus**. This is a game-changer for specialized builds. It allows you to influence the primary stat roll on the crafted item. You can choose to focus on:
1.  **Firearms (Red):** Maximize raw damage output.
2.  **Stamina (Blue):** Increase health and survivability.
3.  **Electronics (Yellow):** Boost skill power and cooldown reduction.

## Recalibration: Fine-Tuning Your Gear

Even with high expertise and tactical augmentations, the "perfect" roll is rare. This is where the Equipment Improvement Station comes into play. Located at the Base of Operations, this station allows you to recalibrate weapons and gear starting at Level 18.

Recalibration in The Division Resurgence follows a strict rule: you can only change **one** attribute or talent on any given piece of gear. Once you choose to change a specific slot, all other slots on that item are locked and cannot be modified.

### Recalibration Priorities
When looking at **the division resurgence weapon mods** and attributes, follow this priority list:

1.  **Talents:** If you have a High-End weapon with a "dead" talent (one that doesn't fit your playstyle), change it first.
2.  **Primary Attributes:** If the damage roll is significantly lower than the maximum, recalibrate it to hit the "God Roll" range.
3.  **Secondary Attributes:** Only recalibrate these if your talents and primary attributes are already ideal.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Abandoning a recalibration result still consumes the required resources. Only confirm the change if it is a definitive upgrade to your current stat.

## FAQ

**Q: Where do I find more Proficiency Points for the division resurgence weapon mods?**

A: Proficiency Points are rewarded for almost every activity. The most efficient way to farm them is by completing Main Missions and Daily Projects. Dismantling high-level gear also provides resources necessary for the crafting process.

**Q: Can I use tactical augmentations on gear like masks and backpacks?**

A: Yes. The Armory covers both weapons and gear. You can use Tactical Augmentations on blueprints for masks, body armor, knee pads, and more to increase their base defensive or utility stats.

**Q: Is it better to find loot in the open world or craft it?**

A: In the early game, open-world loot is sufficient. However, for endgame builds in 2026, crafting is superior because "Crafting Focus" and "Tactical Augmentation" allow you to guarantee specific stats that match your build, which is nearly impossible with random drops.

**Q: What happens if I recalibrate the wrong stat?**

A: Unfortunately, once a stat is selected for recalibration, the other stats on that item are permanently locked. You can recalibrate that *same* slot as many times as you want (provided you have the resources), but you can never change a different slot on that specific item again.
