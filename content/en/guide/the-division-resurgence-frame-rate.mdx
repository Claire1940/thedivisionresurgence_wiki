## Advanced Graphics Optimization for Mobile

To keep a consistent **the division resurgence frame rate**, you shouldn't just rely on the presets. Manual adjustment of post-processing effects can yield a much smoother experience without sacrificing too much visual "pop."

### Key Settings Breakdown

*   **VFX Quality:** Keep this on **Low**. Visual effects like smoke and explosions are the primary cause of frame drops during combat.
*   **Bloom:** Turn this **OFF**. While it adds a nice glow to lights, it consumes unnecessary GPU cycles that are better spent on frame stability.
*   **Anti-Aliasing:** Set this to **ON**. Even on lower resolutions, anti-aliasing helps smooth out jagged edges, making the game look better even when the internal resolution is downscaled.
*   **Volumetric Lighting:** This can be kept **ON** if you have a modern device. It provides the iconic "Division" atmosphere, especially during night cycles and foggy weather.

| Setting | Recommended Value | Performance Impact |
|---|---|---|
| **Resolution** | Standard | High |
| **Graphics Quality** | Medium | Medium |
| **VFX** | Low | High |
| **Dynamic Resolution** | **OFF** | Variable |

> **💡 Tip:** Always turn off **Dynamic Resolution**. While it sounds helpful, it often causes the game to look extremely blurry at the exact moment you need clarity—during a gunfight. It is better to have a slightly lower consistent resolution than one that fluctuates constantly.

## Performance Troubleshooting in The Division Resurgence

Even with the best settings, you might encounter issues with your **the division resurgence frame rate**. If you experience "micro-stutters," check the following:

*   **Thermal Throttling:** Mobile devices will automatically downclock the CPU/GPU if they get too hot. Use a phone cooler or play in a cooler environment for long sessions.
*   **Network Latency:** Sometimes "lag" is mistaken for low FPS. Check your ping in the corner of the screen; a high ping can cause the game to appear as if it is skipping frames.
*   **Background Apps:** Ensure that apps like Discord, screen recorders, or social media are not running in the background, as they compete for the same RAM and CPU cycles.

For more technical support and official updates, visit the [official Ubisoft Division Resurgence site](https://www.ubisoft.com/en-us/game/the-division/resurgence) to check for the latest optimization patches.

## FAQ

**Q: Why can't I select the "High" frame rate on my phone?**

A: If the "High" option is grayed out, your device's chipset may not meet the minimum requirements to sustain 60 FPS. Ensure your phone is not in "Power Saving Mode" and that no other demanding apps are open.

**Q: Does increasing the division resurgence frame rate drain the battery faster?**

A: Yes, moving from 30 FPS to 60 FPS roughly doubles the workload on your GPU, which will lead to significantly faster battery drain and increased heat production.

A: For a shooter like The Division Resurgence, 60 FPS is almost always better. The increased responsiveness and smoother motion make it much easier to track moving targets and react to enemy flanking maneuvers.
