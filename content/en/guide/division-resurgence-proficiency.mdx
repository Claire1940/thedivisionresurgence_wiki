## Understanding the Proficiency System

In The Division Resurgence, proficiency is a specialized progression track tied directly to weapon blueprints. It is distinct from your character level, gear level, and "Gear Expertise." While gear level dictates the raw power of a specific item in your inventory, proficiency dictates the inherent quality of the "Family" of weapons you are using.

When you use a weapon—be it a Vector SMG, a MAS assault rifle, or an Exotic like the Lady Death—you earn experience toward that weapon’s blueprint. As this level increases, you unlock permanent stat boosts that are baked into the weapon's DNA. This means if you reach max proficiency with an M4, every M4 you craft or find in the future will be objectively superior to one used by an agent with lower proficiency.

### Permanent Stat Bonuses

The primary goal of grinding proficiency is to stabilize and sharpen your weapons. The bonuses are incremental but become game-changing at higher tiers.

| Proficiency Level | Primary Stat Bonus | Secondary Stat Bonus |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Level 1-3** | +2% Accuracy | Minor Stability |
| **Level 4-6** | +5% Vertical Stability | Improved Handling |
| **Level 7-9** | +8% Horizontal Stability | Recoil Reduction |
| **Level 10** | **Mastery Bonus** | Optimized Blueprint Stats |

> **💡 Tip:** Proficiency is a marathon, not a sprint. Do not feel pressured to max out every weapon; focus on one primary and one secondary weapon family that fits your specialization.

## How to Increase Division Resurgence Proficiency

The most effective way to level up your proficiency is through active combat. Every kill and mission completion with a weapon equipped contributes to its mastery. However, in 2026, the meta has shifted toward specific activities that provide higher "Mastery XP" yields.

### Best Farming Activities

1.  **Challenging and Heroic Nests:** These are instanced open-world activities that rotate every 24 hours. Heroic Nests offer the highest density of elite enemies, which provide significantly more proficiency XP than standard street mobs.
2.  **The Dark Zone:** While risky, the Dark Zone features high-frequency boss spawns. Eliminating named bosses with your preferred weapon is the fastest way to see those proficiency bars move.
3.  **Daily Quests:** Many daily objectives require specific weapon types (e.g., "Get 20 headshots with an SMG"). These often reward bonus proficiency materials that can be applied to blueprints.

## Gear Level vs. Power Score

It is important to distinguish **division resurgence proficiency** from your Power Score. While proficiency improves the "floor" of your weapon's stats, your Power Score determines your readiness for the game's hardest content.

*   **Gear Level:** Independent of character level. You can take a level 1 item and upgrade it all the way to level 40.
*   **Power Score:** A cumulative value representing your account's strength, influenced by gear levels, mods, achievements, and specialization unlocks.
*   **Proficiency:** A blueprint-specific multiplier that enhances the base performance of a weapon family.

| System | What it Impacts | How to Improve |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Proficiency** | Base Stats (Stability/Accuracy) | Repeated weapon usage |
| **Gear Level** | Raw Damage/Armor | Spending upgrade materials |
| **Power Score** | Content Accessibility | Overall account progression |

## The Role of Damage Types

As you progress into the mid-20s and 30s in level, you will encounter enemies with specific shield types. Your proficiency becomes even more vital here because a stable, accurate weapon makes hitting weak points significantly easier.

Weapons in Resurgence are categorized by three primary damage types:
*   **Red (Blast Damage):** Effective against heavy armor and clusters.
*   **Light Blue (Shredding):** Designed to strip away enemy shields quickly.
*   **Dark Blue (Piercing):** High penetration for taking down armored targets from a distance.

Matching your high-proficiency weapons with the correct damage type is the key to conquering Heroic difficulty content. Always try to keep at least one high-proficiency weapon of each damage type in your inventory.

## Crafting and Blueprint Mastery

Crafting is a core pillar of the game, though it comes with a 24-hour timer for most items. This is where the **division resurgence proficiency** system truly shines. When you craft a new weapon at your workbench, the proficiency bonuses you have earned are applied automatically.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Dismantling a weapon only returns 90% of the invested materials. However, the proficiency progress on the blueprint is permanent and is never lost, even if you dismantle every weapon of that type in your inventory.

### Specialization Synergy

Your choice of specialization (class) should dictate which weapons you focus your proficiency on. For example:
*   **Sharpshooters** should prioritize Marksman Rifle proficiency for the horizontal stability needed for follow-up shots.
*   **Vanguards** should focus on SMG or Shotgun proficiency to ensure maximum accuracy during close-quarters combat.

## Strategic Tips for 2026 Agents

To maximize your efficiency in The Division Resurgence, follow these expert strategies:

*   **Follow the Exclamation Points:** The UI can be overwhelming. Always check menus for "Agent Career" rewards and "Milestone Caches," as these often contain proficiency-boosting materials.
*   **Observe the Daily XP Cap:** There is a daily limit on high-rate XP gains (indicated by an orange arrow). Once this turns gray, your proficiency gains will slow down. Use your "Orange" time for the hardest activities.
*   **Fast Travel to Activities:** Once discovered, you can fast travel directly to open-world activities. This saves time and allows you to hop between Nests to farm weapon XP faster.
*   **Don't Ignore Side Missions:** Unlike The Division 2, side mission XP does not scale. Complete them as soon as you meet the level requirement to get quick boosts to your power score and weapon mastery.

## FAQ

**Q: Does division resurgence proficiency reset if I switch my specialization?**

A: No. Proficiency is tied to the weapon blueprint itself, not your character's class or specialization. You can switch from a Field Medic to a Demolitionist and keep all your weapon bonuses.

**Q: Can I increase proficiency for Exotic weapons?**

A: Yes. Exotic weapons like the Warlord or Lady Death have their own proficiency tracks. Given their high base power, leveling their proficiency is highly recommended for late-game builds.

**Q: Is there a way to bypass the 24-hour crafting timer?**

A: Certain monetization options, such as the "Warden Field Supplies" monthly pass, can reduce crafting time by 40%. However, proficiency levels are earned strictly through gameplay and cannot be purchased directly.

**Q: What happens when I reach level 10 proficiency?**

A: At level 10, you unlock the Mastery Bonus for that blueprint. This provides a significant boost to all core stats of the weapon, making it the most optimized version possible for your current gear level.
