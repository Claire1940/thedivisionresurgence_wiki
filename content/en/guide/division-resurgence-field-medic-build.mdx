## Core Mechanics of the Field Medic Specialization

The Field Medic is not just a "healer" in the classic sense; it is a combat specialist that uses chemical agents to manipulate the battlefield. The specialization excels at "corrosive" damage, a status effect that eats away at enemy armor and health over time. This makes the Field Medic particularly effective against heavily armored bosses and elite units.

The primary gameplay loop involves deploying hives and traps that react to your own weapon fire or the actions of your allies. This synergy requires a proactive playstyle where you aren't just sitting back and watching health bars, but actively engaging targets to trigger your technological drones.

| Feature | Description | Playstyle Impact |
|---|---|---|
| **Role** | War Veteran / Support | High durability and utility |
| **Primary Element** | Corrosion | Effective against armor and shields |
| **Device Type** | Hives and Traps | Area of Effect (AoE) control |
| **Team Synergy** | High | Buffs ally damage and provides minor healing |

> **💡 Tip:** To get the most out of your Field Medic, focus on high-fire-rate weapons like SMGs or Assault Rifles. Many of your skills trigger "per hit," meaning faster weapons deploy drones more frequently.

## Signature Ability: The Oxidizing Swarm

The centerpiece of any **division resurgence field medic build** is the **Oxidizing Swarm**. This is an upgraded version of the standard Oxidizing Hive, containing a massive cache of micro drones filled with a highly potent corrosive substance.

When activated, the swarm identifies all enemies within its radius and launches drones to impact them directly. Upon impact, these drones deal immediate damage and apply a lingering corrosion effect. The ability can be used in two ways:
1. **Quick Tap:** Deploys the device at your feet for immediate self-defense.
2. **Hold and Release:** Allows you to aim and throw the device to a specific location, which is ideal for flushing enemies out of cover.

The signature ability charges over time and gains significant progress when you deal damage to enemies. This encourages an aggressive stance—the more damage you deal, the faster you can redeploy your ultimate corrosive swarm.

## Essential Skills for your Division Resurgence Field Medic Build

To round out your **division resurgence field medic build**, you must understand the two primary skills available to the specialization: the Oxidizing Hive and the Chemical Trap. Both skills offer a unique blend of offensive pressure and defensive utility.

### 1. Oxidizing Hive
This SHADE device contains micro drones that target enemies based on your weapon fire. If you keep the hive attached to your person, every time you hit an enemy, a drone will fly out to strike that same target. Alternatively, you can deploy the hive near an ally. In this mode, the drones will follow that ally and strike whatever they are shooting. This makes it an incredible tool for boosting the DPS of your team's primary damage dealer.

### 2. Chemical Trap
The Chemical Trap is a versatile area-denial tool. When thrown, it splits into a cluster of small containers that shatter on impact, creating a large corrosive zone. 
* **Enemy Effect:** Continuous corrosive damage for anyone standing in the area.
* **Ally Effect:** Teammates standing within the corrosive cloud receive a small healing effect.

| Skill | Primary Use | Key Benefit |
|---|---|---|
| **Oxidizing Hive** | Single Target / Ally Buff | Procs corrosive damage on every weapon hit |
| **Chemical Trap** | Area Denial / Hybrid Support | Damages enemies while simultaneously healing allies |

## Recommended Gear and Attributes

For a successful **division resurgence field medic build**, your gear selection should prioritize Skill Tier and Skill Haste. Since your effectiveness is tied directly to the uptime of your Hive and Trap, reducing cooldowns is your number one priority.

### Priority Attributes
1. **Skill Tier:** Increases the base damage and healing of your corrosive devices.
2. **Skill Haste:** Ensures your skills are off cooldown as often as possible.
3. **Status Effect:** Increases the duration and damage-over-time (DOT) of the corrosion applied by your drones.
4. **Armor:** As a Field Medic, you often need to move between cover to deploy devices or revive allies; a decent armor pool is necessary for survival.

### Weapon Synergies
As mentioned previously, fire rate is king. Look for weapons with the **"In Sync"** talent, which provides a damage boost to both you and your skills when you deal damage. This creates a powerful feedback loop where your weapon buffs your hive, and your hive drones buff your weapon.

> **Warning:** Avoid slow-firing weapons like bolt-action snipers with this build. You will struggle to trigger the micro drones efficiently, significantly lowering your total damage output.

## Tactical Gameplay and Combat Tips

Executing a high-level **division resurgence field medic build** requires more than just gear; it requires tactical awareness. During the "Water is Life" mission, for example, the Field Medic is invaluable for clearing out clusters of "Cleaners" who often bunch up near environmental hazards.

* **The "Pocket" Medic Strategy:** Attach your Oxidizing Hive to a high-armor teammate (like a Vanguard) and let them rush into the fray. Your drones will automatically focus their targets, providing massive fire support while you stay safely in cover.
* **Corrosive Funnels:** Use the Chemical Trap in narrow doorways or hallways. Enemies will be forced to walk through the corrosive gas, softening them up before they even reach your line of sight.
* **Signature Management:** Don't hoard your Oxidizing Swarm. Because it recharges through dealt damage, using it early in an encounter can often result in it being nearly recharged by the time you reach the final boss of a mission.

## FAQ

**Q: Is the Field Medic better for solo or group play?**

A: While the Field Medic is a powerhouse in groups due to its ally-buffing drones, it is surprisingly strong for solo play. The Chemical Trap provides much-needed self-healing, and the Oxidizing Hive acts as a "second shooter," significantly increasing your solo DPS.

**Q: How do I unlock the Field Medic specialization?**

A: In the 2026 version of the game, the Field Medic is typically unlocked through the specialization tutorial missions once you reach the main campaign's mid-point. Check your specialization desk in the SHADE hub for specific requirements.

**Q: Does the "corrosion" effect stack with other status effects?**

A: Yes, corrosion is a unique status effect. You can combine it with fire (from incendiary grenades) or bleed for devastating damage-over-time results. This is a core strategy for an advanced **division resurgence field medic build**.

**Q: Can the Chemical Trap heal me as well as my allies?**

A: Yes, the Chemical Trap provides a small healing effect to any friendly unit standing within its radius, including the player who deployed it. However, the healing is secondary to its corrosive damage, so it should not be your only source of sustain in high-difficulty content.
