## SHD Recruitment: The Virtual Pre-Testing Session

Before you are deployed into the field, every recruit must pass a virtual simulation. This serves as the game's tutorial, teaching you the fundamentals of movement, gunplay, and the signature cover system that defines the series. You will be evaluated on your ability to handle various weapon platforms and your tactical awareness under pressure.

### Basic Training Objectives
The simulation is divided into several modules. Pay close attention to the environmental cues, as the skills learned here are vital for the high-stakes encounters in the open world.

| Module | Objective | Key Skill Learned |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Body Awareness** | Navigate the obstacle course | Sprinting, vaulting, and camera control |
| **Marksmanship** | Neutralize static and moving targets | Recoil management and critical hit zones |
| **Cover Tactics** | Advance past a hostile turret | Cover-to-cover movement and timing |
| **Specialization** | Utilize SHD tech devices | Skill deployment and Signature abilities |

> **💡 Tip:** Use the "Cover-to-Cover" mechanic by holding the movement button while looking at a new piece of cover. This reduces the damage you take while moving between positions compared to simply running in the open.

## Choosing Your Specialization

Once the basics are mastered, you must select a specialization. In 2026, the meta for *The Division Resurgence* favors versatility, but your choice should reflect your preferred playstyle. | Specialization | Primary Role | Core Skills |

## Prologue: The Spring Blossom Restaurant

Your first real-world assignment involves extracting a high-value target (VIP) named Angela Chan from Chinatown. Local community leaders are vital to the rebuilding effort, and the JTF (Joint Task Force) has lost control of the area. You will link up with Agent Kelly at the Spring Blossom Restaurant to secure the perimeter.

### Mission Steps:
1. **Reach the Restaurant:** Move through the alleys of Chinatown. You will encounter small groups of rioters. Practice your headshots here to conserve ammo.
2. **Defend the VIP:** Once you meet Angela Chan and her nephew Kevin, a wave of hostiles will assault the restaurant. Use the elevated positions near the entrance to maintain a line of sight on the street.
3. **Escort to Checkpoint:** You must move the civilians to the Manhattan Bridge checkpoint. The JTF is overwhelmed, so expect "purple" (Veteran) enemies to flank your position.
4. **Weak Point Targeting:** During the final stand at the bridge, enemies with backpacks or explosive pouches will appear. Aiming for these weak points will cause area-of-effect damage, making the crowd control much easier.

## Act 1: The Trinity Church Crisis

The situation in Lower Manhattan deteriorates quickly as a new hostile leader named Jax begins operating with heavy weaponry. Your next major objective in this **the division resurgence walkthrough** is the Trinity Church Field Hospital, which has come under heavy attack.

### The Stock Exchange and the Elevator
To reach the church, you must navigate through the New York Stock Exchange. This area introduces verticality and tighter corridors. 

*   **The Elevator Puzzle:** You will need to defend your position while Agent Kelly repairs a malfunctioning elevator. Use grenades to keep enemies from rushing your cover.
*   **Heavy Weapons Awareness:** Be advised that the hostiles have looted JTF supplies, including .50 cal machine guns and recoilless rifles. If you see a red laser or a telegraph on the ground, move immediately.

### Boss Fight: Carl and the Milkshake
Inside the Stock Exchange, you will encounter Carl, a henchman for Jax. Carl is a "tank" style enemy with high armor. 
*   **Strategy:** Carl moves slowly but hits hard. Use your Signature ability (if playing Vanguard, the damage boost) to shred his armor plates. Stay mobile and don't let him corner you in the narrow teller aisles.

## The Battle for Liberty Island

The climax of the prologue takes place at the iconic Statue of Liberty. Jax has taken Doc O'Neal hostage and plans to blow up the supplies intended for the survivors. This mission tests everything you have learned in this **the division resurgence walkthrough**.

### Defusing the Charges
Unlike standard combat missions, Liberty Island features an EOD (Explosive Ordnance Disposal) mechanic. You must interact with the bombs while your partner provides covering fire. 
1. **Clear the Ferry Dock:** Neutralize the snipers on the upper decks of the ferry.
2. **Disarm the Statue Base:** There are multiple charges rigged to the doors holding the hostages. You must stay within the interaction zone to diffuse them. If you take damage, the timer pauses, so ensure the area is clear before starting.

> **Warning:** Jax utilizes a minigun during the final encounter. Do not attempt to trade fire with him while he is revving up. Wait for his reload animation to deal damage.

## Post-Prologue: The One-Month Time Skip

After the events at Liberty Island, your agent is injured and falls into a coma. You wake up one month later at the Base of Operations (BoO) in Midtown. The world has changed; the First Wave has largely vanished, and the Second Wave has been activated.

### Meeting Fay Lau
Agent Fay Lau will brief you on the current state of Manhattan. The city is carved up between several factions:
*   **The Rikers:** Escaped convicts from Riker's Island.
*   **The Cleaners:** Former sanitation workers obsessed with burning the virus out of the city.
*   **The Last Man Battalion (LMB):** A rogue private military company.
*   **The Freemen:** A new, decentralized faction of survivalists.

### The Spring Blossom Settlement
Your primary focus shifts to building a home for the civilians you rescued. The Spring Blossom Settlement serves as your social hub and gear upgrade station. Here, you can meet NPCs like Angela Chan and Moss (the handyman) to unlock new wings of the base.

| Settlement Wing | Benefit | Upgrade Priority |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Medical Clinic** | Unlocks better healing kits and virus resistance | **High** |
| **Power Grid** | Unlocks advanced SHD tech gadgets | **Medium** |
| **Rooftop Stockpile** | Increases crafting material yields | **Low** |

## Advanced Survival Tips for 2026

To truly master the streets, you need to look beyond the basic mission objectives. Manhattan is full of hidden secrets and tactical advantages.

*   **Gear Leveling:** Use your worn metal components and fabric to level up your blue (Rare) gear early. Don't hoard materials, as you will find plenty of upgrades as you progress through Act 2.
*   **Echoes and Intel:** Always activate Echos when you find them. They provide valuable XP and context for the rogue agents like Aaron Keener who are still at large.
*   **Performance Settings:** If you experience frame drops during intense firefights, switch to "45 FPS / High Graphics" or "60 FPS / Medium Graphics" in the settings menu. Maintaining a steady frame rate is crucial for landing headshots.
*   **Inventory Management:** Periodically deconstruct old gear rather than selling it. The crafting materials are far more valuable than the credits in the early game.

## FAQ

**Q: Is The Division Resurgence a prequel to the first game?**

A: It acts as both a prequel and a parallel story. You play as a First Wave agent, witnessing the initial collapse and the events that led to the activation of the Second Wave seen in the original game.

**Q: Can I complete the division resurgence walkthrough entirely solo?**

A: Yes, the main campaign is designed to be solo-friendly. However, certain endgame activities and Dark Zone extractions are significantly easier with a group.

**Q: How do I unlock new Specializations?**

A: You choose your first specialization during the tutorial. Additional specializations are unlocked as you progress through the main story and reach higher agent levels at the Base of Operations.

**Q: What happened to the First Wave agents?**

A: As explained by Fay Lau, many First Wave agents went MIA, KIA, or Rogue (joining Aaron Keener) in the Dark Zone. Your character is one of the few loyal First Wave agents remaining.
