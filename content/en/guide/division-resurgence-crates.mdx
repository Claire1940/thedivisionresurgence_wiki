## Why Hunt for SHD Crates?

Unlike standard loot containers found in missions, SHD crates are specialized caches that focus on end-game progression and specialization power. Each crate typically contains a guaranteed chip set part, which is the primary currency for upgrading your specialization gear, such as the Grenade Launcher or Seekermines.

| Reward Type | Typical Quantity | Rarity | Purpose |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Chip Set Parts** | 1 | **Purple (Superior)** | Upgrading Specialization Abilities |
| **Phoenix Credits** | 40 | **High** | Purchasing premium gear and blueprints |
| **Regular Credits** | 5,000 | Standard | General vendor purchases and crafting |
| **Crafting Materials** | 1-3 | Varied | Fabric, Metal, and Warm Components |

> **💡 Tip:** Even if you receive a chip set part for a specialization you don't currently use, do not discard it. You can break these down to gain materials for the specific specialization you are currently leveling.

## Navigation and Fast Travel Tips

Before you set out on your scavenger hunt for **division resurgence crates**, it is important to understand the map's layout. The New York map is divided into districts, and while the icons for crates may appear once you are close, they do not always indicate the verticality required to reach them.

1.  **Use the Base of Operations (BoO):** Many crates are clustered around the central hub. Use this as your primary fast-travel point.
2.  **Checkpoints and Outposts:** If you have cleared a generator mission or an outpost, you can fast travel directly to these icons to shave minutes off your travel time.
3.  **Climbing Mechanics:** Most crates are not hidden behind complex puzzles. If you see a yellow tarp, a ladder, or a stack of crates, you can usually vault or climb over them to find a hidden path.

## SHD Crate Locations 1 through 5

The first set of crates is located in the relatively safe "Light Zone" surrounding your initial base. These are excellent for early-game players (levels 10-15) to jumpstart their power levels.

### Crate #1: The BoO Southwest Corner
Located just southwest of the New York Base of Operations. Look for a large red truck parked near a lot full of civilian cars. Head past the truck and look for an alleyway. You will need to climb a ladder to reach the gantry where the crate sits.

### Crate #2: The Circular Building
On the northwest corner of the large circular building adjacent to the BoO. Locate the yellow forklift. You can climb over the forklift to access a fenced-in area. The crate is tucked inside this small enclosure.

### Crate #3: The Eastern Gantry
Travel directly east along the road situated above the BoO. On your left, you will find an alleyway. Look for two white vans parked side-by-side. Climb onto the roof of the vans to reach the metal gantry above, where the third of the **division resurgence crates** is located.

### Crate #4: Tenderloin District
This crate is near the edge of the Dark Zone in the Tenderloin district. It is often located near an active generator mission. Look for a cluster of military trucks and a burning police car. The crate is sitting on top of a red shipping container in the center of the clearing.

### Crate #5: Chelsea Park
Head to the southwest area of Chelsea. You will find a large park area with dry fountains. Locate the "All American Liquor" store sign. Behind this building is an alleyway filled with trash piles. Use the ladder on the side of the building to reach the roof gantry for the prize.

## SHD Crate Locations 6 through 10

As you move further from the center, the locations of **division resurgence crates** become slightly more obscured by the environment.

| Crate # | Location | Landmark | Reachability |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **6** | Chelsea Courtyard | Blue Barriers | **Unreachable** (Current Bug) |
| **7** | West BoO Area | JTF Green Truck | High (Ladder/Vault) |
| **8** | Railway Bridge | Footbridge Overpass | Medium (Running Path) |
| **9** | Gas Station Block | Yellow Digger | High (Container Roof) |
| **10** | Hell's Kitchen | Blue Containers | High (Courtyard) |

### Crate #6: The Courtyard (Warning)
This crate is located in a courtyard near Crate #5, accessible through a series of stone columns. However, as of the current 2026 build, this crate is often blocked by blue barriers that lack a climb prompt. Most players consider this crate currently bugged or mission-locked.

### Crate #7: The JTF Truck
Back near the BoO, head west toward the construction area under the bridge. Find the small alley with a green JTF truck. Climb the crates next to the truck to get onto its roof, then vault onto the fire escape to find the crate.

### Crate #8: The Railway Overpass
Very close to Crate #7, look for the stairs leading up to the elevated railway. Follow the tracks until you reach a small pedestrian footbridge on your right. The crate is sitting on the bridge platform.

### Crate #9: The Yellow Digger
Head one block south from the gas station on the western side of the map. Look for a large yellow construction digger. The crate is located on top of a small utility hut directly behind the heavy machinery.

### Crate #10: Hell's Kitchen Edge
Travel to the far northwest corner of the map, right at the edge of the playable zone near the water. Enter the large courtyard filled with blue shipping containers. You will need to climb to the very top of the stacked containers to find the SHD cache.

## Final Crates: 11 through 13

The final three **division resurgence crates** require a bit more travel but offer the same high-quality specialization rewards.

### Crate #11: The Military Outpost
Move slightly northeast from Hell's Kitchen, staying along the map's edge. This crate is hidden behind a green military hut near a checkpoint. Climb the wooden crates stacked against the back of the hut to reach the roof.

### Crate #12: The Playground
From Crate #11, head due east across the main junction. You will find a small playground with slides. At the back of the park, there is a ladder leading to the rooftops. Follow the roof path to the sloped section to find the container.

### Crate #13: The Theater Balcony
This is arguably the hardest crate to find. Located south of Times Square on a road filled with abandoned buses. Look for a "cherry picker" lift near a theater building. You must climb onto a parked van, vault onto a small box, and then jump to the theater’s front balcony. The crate is located on the gantry above the entrance.

## FAQ

**Q: Do division resurgence crates respawn?**

A: SHD crates are generally one-time pickups per account or character. Once you have collected the chip set part and Phoenix credits from a specific location, the crate will remain open and cannot be looted again.

**Q: Are the rewards level-scaled?**

A: While some basic materials may scale with your level, the core rewards—Purple Chip Set parts and 40 Phoenix Credits—appear to be fixed rewards designed to help players progress their specializations regardless of their current gear score.

**Q: Why can't I reach Crate #6?**

A: Crate #6 in the Chelsea courtyard has been reported as unreachable by many players due to a missing climb prompt on the blue barriers. It is recommended to check this location after major game updates in 2026 to see if the path has been cleared.

**Q: Is there a map icon for these crates?**

A: These are considered "Secret" or "Hidden" crates. They typically do not show up on your world map until you are in very close proximity, making guides like this essential for finding them all.
