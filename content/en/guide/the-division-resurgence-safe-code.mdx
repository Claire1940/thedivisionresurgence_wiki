## Tips for Finding More The Division Resurgence Safe Codes

While this guide covers the most prominent and easily accessible safes, the world of *The Division Resurgence* is expansive, and new secrets are often discovered as new sectors open up or patches are released. If you encounter a safe not listed here, follow these investigative steps to uncover its code:

*   **Scan the Walls and Surroundings:** Developers often hide codes in plain sight on graffiti, posters, chalkboards, or even small notes. Look for anything that resembles a sequence of numbers.
*   **Listen for ISAC Cues:** Your trusty ISAC system is designed to help. It will often announce "Object of Interest Detected" or "Portable Electronic Device Detected" when you are near a clue or a safe, guiding your search.
*   **Look for Collectibles:** If a safe doesn't have an obvious number nearby, search for a blue or gold collectible glow. These "Cryptic Clues" are stored in your inventory and contain the hints or riddles needed for the keypad.
*   **Adjust Brightness Settings:** If a code on a sign or poster appears too blurry or faded to read, temporarily increasing your graphical resolution or brightness in the game's settings menu can often make the numbers pop out more clearly.

> **💡 Tip:** Always keep a few inventory slots open before embarking on a looting run. There’s nothing more frustrating than cracking a high-level safe only to realize you have no room for its valuable rewards!

## Reward Tiers and Scaling from Safes

The rewards obtained by using **the Division Resurgence safe code** combinations typically scale with your character's level, though the rarity (color) of the items is usually fixed for specific safes.

*   **Blue (Specialized):** These are standard rewards often found in more common, street-level safes. They provide a solid boost in the early game.
*   **Purple (Superior):** These higher-tier items are found in settlements and more hidden areas, such as the Serotentia safe. Superior gear offers significant stat improvements.
*   **Resources:** Most safes provide a combination of "E-Credits," weapon parts, and fabric, which are crucial for crafting, upgrading, and recalibrating your gear.

For new players, the immediate power boost from a Superior weapon or gear piece often outweighs the benefit of waiting. However, if you're looking to maximize your Power Score, it can be beneficial to wait until you are at the level cap for a specific region before opening safes, as the gear will then drop at the highest possible score.
