## Weapon Leveling and Power Score Mechanics

In Division Resurgence, your weapon's level is independent of your character's level. This is a revolutionary change that allows you to keep a favorite "god-roll" weapon from level 1 all the way to level 40. By investing upgrade materials, you can scale the base stats of your firearm to keep pace with the escalating difficulty of the campaign.

However, keep in mind that your **Power Score**—the metric that determines your readiness for Heroic Nests and Dark Zone tiers—is influenced by more than just the weapon's level.

1.  **Weapon Mods:** Scopes, magazines, and underbarrel attachments provide flat stat boosts and contribute significantly to your total Power Score.
2.  **OS Protocols:** These high-level upgrades are essential for endgame viability but require significant resource investment.
3.  **Rarity Tiers:** While a level 40 Green weapon is possible, a level 40 High-End weapon will always possess superior base attributes and additional talent slots.

> **⚠️ Warning:** When dismantling weapons to recover materials, you only receive 90% of the invested resources. For OS Protocols, this return drops to 80%. Choose your upgrades wisely to avoid resource depletion.

## How to Farm High-End Weapons

Securing the **best weapons division resurgence** offers requires a strategic approach to daily activities. Because the game implements a daily XP cap and a limit on open-world activity rewards (currently 10 per day), you must prioritize high-value targets.

*   **Daily Quests:** The most consistent source of materials and XP. Use your 5 free rerolls to target weapon-specific rewards.
*   **Heroic Nests:** These instanced challenges rotate every 24 hours. They are the primary source for High-End weapon blueprints and mods.
*   **The Dark Zone:** High risk, high reward. Bosses in the Dark Zone have the highest drop rate for "naturally" leveled High-End gear, bypassing the need for extensive crafting.
*   **Agent Career Track:** Following the objectives in your Agent Career menu provides milestone caches that often contain guaranteed High-End weapons.

## The Role of Specializations

Your choice of specialization (class) heavily dictates which weapons will perform best for you. While you can switch specializations at the Base of Operations, your weapon talents should ideally complement your active skills.

*   **Vanguard:** Excels with Assault Rifles and Shotguns. Look for talents that proc on close-range kills.
*   **Demolitionist:** Synergizes perfectly with "Red" Blast damage weapons.
*   **Field Medic:** Benefits from weapons with high stability, allowing for consistent fire while managing team heals.

In the current state of the game, crafting is often viewed as a secondary system rather than a primary source of gear. The 24-hour real-time timer for crafting even basic items makes it a slow process. Most top-tier players recommend using crafting only to fill specific gaps in a build rather than attempting to "roll" for the perfect weapon.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the single best weapon for a new player in Division Resurgence?**

A: The **Warlord Exotic Assault Rifle** is widely considered the best starting choice if you have access to the Classified Ops Pass. If playing entirely for free, any High-End Assault Rifle with "Shredding" (Light Blue) damage will provide the most versatility during the early campaign.

**Q: Can I use my level 10 Gold weapon when I am level 40?**

A: Yes. You can use the upgrade system to increase the weapon's level alongside your character. As long as you have the required materials, a weapon can be scaled to the level cap, though you may eventually find "Tiered" gear in the endgame that offers better stat ceilings.

**Q: How do I unlock more weapon mod slots?**

A: Mod slots are determined by the rarity of the weapon. High-End (Gold) weapons typically feature the maximum number of slots. To fill them, you must farm Nests or purchase mod caches from the specialization vendor.

**Q: Does the daily XP cap affect weapon drops?**

A: No. While the daily XP cap slows your character progression, it does not prevent you from looting weapons. However, you are limited to rewards from 10 open-world activities per day, so focus those 10 slots on activities that list "Weapon Caches" as a reward.
