## Unlocking Blueprints and the Armory System

Before you can craft anything in The Division Resurgence, you'll need to acquire blueprints. These foundational schematics dictate what gear and weapons you can produce. The primary hub for managing your blueprints is the Armory, accessible via the Phoenix icon in the top-right corner of your character menu.

Within the Armory, you'll find an extensive catalog of weapons (SMGs, LMGs, Assault Rifles, Marksman Rifles, Shotguns, Exotics) and gear pieces (Holsters, Gloves, Backpacks, Knee Pads, Body Armor, Masks). Initially, not all blueprints will be available; many are gated by your agent's level progression. However, a significant selection is accessible from the early stages of the game.

Blueprints are primarily unlocked using **Proficiency Points**. These points are earned continuously through almost any activity you engage in within the game, so you don't need to specifically farm for them. They accumulate naturally as you play. To unlock a blueprint, simply navigate to the desired item in the Armory and spend the required Proficiency Points.

A unique aspect of the Armory system is its progression tree. Once you unlock a blueprint, you'll see a path to related items. For instance, unlocking the "Sausage 12" shotgun might make the "ACS 12" available. Furthermore, reaching a **Crafting Expertise Level** of 5 on a specific blueprint will automatically unlock the next item in its progression line, saving you Proficiency Points for other blueprints. While you could rely on this automatic unlock, it's often advisable to spend Proficiency Points on blueprints for weapons and gear you genuinely intend to use, ensuring they are readily available.

| Blueprint Type | Example Cost (Proficiency Points) | Unlock Condition | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Common Weapon | **2,000** | Available early game | Initial blueprints to get started. |
| Common Gear | **1,500** | Available early game | Essential for basic loadouts. |
| Advanced Weapon | **3,000 - 5,000+** | Level progression, previous blueprint Expertise Level 5 | Unlocks more powerful variants. |

| Exotic Items | **Varies, high** | Specific quests, high-level progression | End-game powerful items. |

> **💡 Tip:** Prioritize unlocking blueprints for weapons and gear that align with your preferred playstyle. Don't waste Proficiency Points on items you'll never use.

## Tactical Augmentation: Enhancing Your Blueprints

Beyond simply unlocking blueprints, The Division Resurgence allows you to further enhance them through **Tactical Augmentation**. This system enables you to upgrade a blueprint itself, meaning any item crafted from that blueprint will benefit from the applied augmentations.

Tactical Augmentation items are typically acquired as rewards from boss encounters or other high-value activities. When you obtain an augmentation, you can equip it to a specific blueprint in the Armory. These augmentations come in slots, offering various benefits:

*   **Bonus Attributes:** An augmentation might increase a specific bonus attribute of the crafted item by a percentage (e.g., +30% bonus attributes). Multiple slots can further boost this, potentially reaching +60% or more.
*   **Talent Levels:** Some augmentations can increase the level of talents on the crafted weapon, making their effects more potent.

It's crucial to understand that tactical augmentations are applied to the *blueprint*, not individual crafted items. If you craft a weapon, then later augment its blueprint, the already-crafted weapon will *not* automatically update. You would need to craft a new item from the augmented blueprint to gain the benefits. This makes careful planning important, especially for your preferred weapons and gear.

| Augmentation Type | Effect on Crafted Item | Acquisition Method | Strategic Importance |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bonus Attribute Slot 1 | Increases bonus attributes by **+30%** | Boss rewards, high-tier activities | Significant early boost to item stats. |
| Bonus Attribute Slot 2 | Increases bonus attributes by **+60%** | Rare boss drops, end-game content | Maximizes primary attribute rolls. |
| Talent Level Increase | Enhances specific weapon/gear talents | Challenging activities, special events | Makes talents more effective and build-defining. |

## Crafting at the Base of Operations

Once you have your desired blueprints unlocked and potentially augmented, the next step in this **The Division Resurgence crafting guide** is to head to the crafting stations at your Base of Operations. You'll find separate stations for weapon crafting and gear crafting.

Interacting with a crafting station brings up a menu displaying all your unlocked blueprints. Here, you'll encounter the **Crafting Expertise Level** for each blueprint. This level is critical because it directly influences the rarity and potential stats of the item you craft.

*   **Increasing Crafting Expertise:** You increase a blueprint's Crafting Expertise Level by spending Proficiency Points. Each level-up costs a certain amount of points (e.g., 2,250 Proficiency Points per level).
*   **Impact on Rarity:** As your Crafting Expertise Level increases, the probability of crafting higher-rarity items (Epic, High-End) improves, while the chance of common or rare items decreases. For example, at Expertise Level 6, you might have a 38% chance for Common/Rare, 19% for Epic, and 5% for High-End. Reaching higher expertise levels can shift these percentages significantly, making High-End items much more likely.
*   **Attribute Range:** A higher Crafting Expertise Level also broadens the potential range of attributes on the crafted item, making it more likely to roll with higher values. Once a blueprint reaches its maximum expertise level, the attributes on crafted items will be close to their "God-rolled" potential.

| Crafting Expertise Level | Common Chance | Rare Chance | Epic Chance | High-End Chance |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Level 1** | **50%** | **40%** | **9%** | **1%** |
| **Level 6** | **38%** | **38%** | **19%** | **5%** |
| **Level 10+** | **Lower** | **Lower** | **Higher** | **Significantly Higher** |

### Crafting Focus

At Crafting Expertise Level 15, you unlock **Crafting Focus**. This advanced feature allows you to influence the primary attribute of your crafted item. When using Crafting Focus, you can select one of three categories:

*   **Firearms (Red):** Increases the likelihood and range of Firearms attributes.
*   **Stamina (Blue):** Increases the likelihood and range of Stamina attributes.
*   **Electronics (Yellow):** Increases the likelihood and range of Electronics attributes.

By selecting a focus, you can steer the crafting outcome towards the stats most beneficial for your build, for example, ensuring your weapon rolls with a higher Firearms score.

### The Crafting Process

Once you're ready, click "Start Crafting." The process is time-gated, typically taking several minutes (e.g., six minutes). You can leave the Base of Operations and continue playing while your item is being crafted. You'll receive an in-game mail notification when it's complete.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Crafting an item consumes no resources other than time. If you're not satisfied with the outcome (e.g., low rarity or undesirable talents), you can dismantle it and craft another one from the same blueprint. This allows for repeated attempts to get the perfect roll, especially once your blueprint's expertise is high.

## The Recalibration System

Even with a perfect crafting process, sometimes you need to fine-tune your gear further. This is where the **Recalibration System** comes into play. Available at the Equipment Improvement Station (located near the crafting benches), recalibration allows you to modify specific attributes or talents on existing gear and weapons. This system unlocks once your agent reaches **Level 18**.

Recalibration in The Division Resurgence functions similarly to its predecessors:

*   **One Change Per Item:** The most critical rule is that you can only recalibrate *one* attribute or talent on any given item. Once a change is confirmed, that slot is locked, and you cannot change any other part of that item. However, you can re-recalibrate the *same* chosen slot multiple times if you're not happy with the initial outcome.
*   **Selecting What to Change:** When you enter the recalibration menu, you can inspect an item's talents and attributes. Clicking the "I" symbol next to a talent or attribute will show you its current effect and a list of possible alternative talents/attributes you could roll.
*   **Resources:** Recalibration requires specific resources, which you'll need to farm through gameplay. The type and quantity of resources depend on the rarity and type of item being recalibrated.
*   **Trial and Error:** You can attempt to recalibrate a chosen slot multiple times. Each attempt costs resources and presents a new roll from the possible options. If you're unhappy with a new roll, you can abandon it, but the resources used for that attempt will still be consumed. If you accept a new roll, it replaces the old one.

| Recalibration Target | Effect | Cost | Strategy |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Weapon Talent** | Changes one weapon talent to a random alternative from a pool. | Specific recalibration resources | Ideal for fine-tuning weapon synergies with your build. |
| **Gear Talent** | Changes one gear talent to a random alternative from a pool. | Specific recalibration resources | Essential for completing gear set bonuses or optimizing utility. |
| **Weapon Attribute** | Changes one weapon attribute (e.g., damage, crit chance) to a random value within its range. | Specific recalibration resources | Use if a particular attribute is significantly low or undesirable. |
| **Gear Attribute** | Changes one gear attribute (e.g., health, status effect duration) to a random value within its range. | Specific recalibration resources | Perfect for min-maxing your defensive or utility stats. |

> **💡 Tip:** Always aim to find gear or weapons that are *almost* perfect for your build, needing only one recalibration to become ideal. This conserves resources and streamlines your optimization process.

## Optimizing Your Crafting and Recalibration Strategy

Mastering **The Division Resurgence crafting guide** involves a strategic approach that combines smart blueprint management, expertise development, and precise recalibration.

1.  **Prioritize Blueprints for Core Build:** Identify the weapons and gear pieces that are central to your desired build. Focus your Proficiency Points on unlocking and leveling up the Crafting Expertise of these specific blueprints first.
2.  **Leverage Tactical Augmentations Early:** If you acquire Tactical Augmentation items for your core blueprints, apply them as soon as possible. Remember that these enhancements affect *newly crafted* items, so augmenting before extensive crafting saves you time and resources in the long run.
3.  **Max Out Crafting Expertise:** Before attempting to craft your "final" gear pieces, try to get your blueprint's Crafting Expertise Level as high as possible. This significantly increases your chances of rolling High-End items with near-maximum attributes, reducing the need for costly recalibrations later.
4.  **Use Crafting Focus Wisely:** Once unlocked, always use Crafting Focus when crafting your preferred items. Directing the primary attribute roll (Firearms, Stamina, Electronics) ensures the item is more likely to fit your build's stat requirements.
5.  **Recalibrate for Perfection:** Use recalibration as the final step in optimizing an item. Since you can only change one aspect, save it for the talent or attribute that is furthest from your ideal roll. Don't be afraid to re-recalibrate the same slot if the initial attempts don't yield the desired outcome, provided you have the resources.
6.  **Don't Over-Invest in Open-World Drops (Initially):** While you can recalibrate any item, including those found in the open world, crafted items with high blueprint expertise often provide a more consistent path to "God-rolled" stats. Use open-world drops as temporary placeholders or for their inherent talents, then transition to expertly crafted gear.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I get Proficiency Points for crafting in The Division Resurgence?**

A: Proficiency Points are earned automatically by engaging in almost any activity within the game. You don't need to focus on specific farming methods; simply playing the game and completing missions, activities, and encounters will yield these points for your **The Division Resurgence crafting guide** endeavors.

**Q: Can I speed up the crafting time in The Division Resurgence?**

A: In the current beta, there isn't an apparent way to speed up the crafting timer. You'll need to wait the allotted time (typically several minutes) for your item to be completed. It's common for mobile games to offer options to speed this up, so this might be introduced in the full release.

**Q: What is the benefit of increasing a blueprint's Crafting Expertise Level?**

A: Increasing a blueprint's Crafting Expertise Level significantly improves the chances of crafting higher-rarity items (Epic, High-End) and increases the potential range of attributes on the crafted items. This means you're more likely to get a powerful item with better stats.

**Q: If I recalibrate an item and don't like the new roll, can I go back to the old one?**

A: No, once you attempt a recalibration and a new roll is presented, you must either accept it or abandon it. If you abandon it, the previous attribute/talent will remain, but the recalibration resources used for that attempt are still consumed. You cannot revert to a previous roll after accepting a new one.
