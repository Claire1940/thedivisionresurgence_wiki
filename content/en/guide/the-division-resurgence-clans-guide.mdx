## How to Join and Create a Clan

Before you can access the specialized quarters, you must first unlock the clan system. This typically occurs early in the campaign via the "Home Away From Home" side mission involving Roy Benitez at the Base of Operations. Roy acts as the Clan Coordinator, facilitating the search for existing groups or the founding of a new one.

### Creating Your Own Clan
If you have the leadership qualities to run a cell, creating a clan requires a one-time investment of **300 Phoenix Credits**. When naming your clan, keep in mind that the system currently prohibits special characters and numbers, and spaces may be restricted depending on your current patch version. Once created, you become the Clan Leader, giving you the authority to manage promotions, set fundraising priorities, and customize the clan banner.

### Joining an Existing Clan
For agents who prefer to jump straight into the action, the Clan Search function allows you to filter through active groups. Joining a clan is character-specific, meaning you may need to rejoin or find different groups if you are running multiple alt characters, though most high-level clans prefer dedicated mains.

| Feature | Requirement / Cost |
|---|---|
| **Unlock Mission** | Home Away From Home (Roy Benitez) |
| **Creation Cost** | **300 Phoenix Credits** |
| **Initial Member Cap** | 30 Members |
| **Naming Restrictions** | No numbers or special characters |

## The Clan Base and Facility Progression

Once you are part of a clan, you gain access to a dedicated instanced area known as the Clan Base. This is your "home away from home" where you and your teammates contribute resources to build out essential infrastructure. The progression of your clan is tied to the **Central Power Hub**. By upgrading this hub, you increase the overall Clan Level, which in turn unlocks new facilities.

### Facility Unlock Schedule
Upgrading your clan isn't just about prestige; it’s about utility. Higher-level clans have access to tools that solo players simply cannot replicate at the standard Base of Operations.

| Clan Level | Facility Unlocked | Function |
|---|---|---|
| **Level 1** | Clan Vendor | Purchase rare gear, outfits, and mods |
| **Level 2** | Gear Crafting Station | Specialized armor blueprints and crafting |
| **Level 4** | Weapon Crafting Station | Exclusive weapon manufacturing |
| **Level 6** | Dismantling Machine | Enhanced resource recovery from gear |

> **💡 Tip:** Clan Leaders can set a "Fundraising Suggestion." Always check the Clan Base Coordinator NPC to see which facility the leader wants to prioritize so the group progresses efficiently.

## Clan Resources and the Economy

The clan system operates on a dual-currency loop: **Clan Base Supplies** (often represented by stars) and **Clan Credits**. Understanding how to flip these resources is the key to getting the best gear in the game.

1.  **Earning Clan Base Supplies:** You earn these supplies by completing almost any activity in the game while being a member of a clan. This includes main story missions, world events, and Dark Zone extractions.
2.  **Donating to Facilities:** Take your supplies to the Clan Base Coordinator. By "donating" these supplies to a specific facility (like the Power Hub or the Vendor), you contribute to the clan's experience points.
3.  **Receiving Clan Credits:** When you donate supplies, you are rewarded with Clan Credits. This is a personal currency that you can spend at the Clan Vendor.
4.  **Shopping at the Vendor:** The Clan Vendor stocks items that are often superior to standard world drops. This includes **High-End Augmentation Modules**, exotic equipment components, and exclusive cosmetic outfits.

### Clan Vendor Level Requirements
Items in the Clan Vendor are often gated by your personal character level. For example, while you might see a powerful weapon at the vendor, you may need to be Level 12 or Level 25 to actually purchase and equip it. These stocks reset periodically, so it is wise to check the vendor daily for rare blueprints or modules.

## Team Bonuses and Social Buffs

One of the strongest arguments for using this **the division resurgence clans guide** to optimize your group play is the passive "Team Bonus." When you are in a squad with fellow clan members, you receive a significant buff to your core combat stats.

These bonuses scale based on how many clan members are in your party and the current level of your clan's facilities. The primary buffs include:
*   **Firepower:** Increases your overall DPS and weapon handling.
*   **Toughness:** Boosts your health pool and armor mitigation.
*   **Engineering:** Improves skill power and gadget cooldowns.
*   **XP Rates:** Provides a percentage-based increase to all experience earned, making the grind to the level cap much faster.

> **Warning:** These bonuses are only active when playing in a group with members of your specific clan. They do not apply if you are matchmade with random players.

## Weekly Projects: Enemy Nests and Clues

To keep the clan active, *The Division Resurgence* features a weekly project system. Currently, clans are tasked with completing up to 60 activities per week to max out rewards. The most prominent of these activities is the **Enemy Nest** hunt.

### The Clue System
In the open world, you will encounter Enemy Nests. Clearing these provides you with a "Clue." You take this clue back to the **Clan Base Intel Officer** and submit it to the Clue Board. 

*   **Solo Submissions:** If you submit a clue alone, the Intel Officer will give you a general location of a Hostile Nest on your map. This location is usually marked by a very large search radius, making the nest harder to find.
*   **Group Submissions:** If you and three other clan mates (a full squad of four) all collect clues and submit them together, the search radius on the map becomes significantly smaller. This allows you to pinpoint the nest almost instantly.

Finding and clearing the Hostile Nest marked by the Clue Board rewards the entire participating group with a massive amount of **Clan Base Supplies** (often upwards of 5,000 per run). This is the most efficient way to farm the resources needed to level up your clan facilities quickly.

## Best Practices for Clan Management

If you are the leader of a clan in 2026, your primary goal is retention and steady growth. Because the facility levels require tens of thousands of resources, a dead clan will quickly fall behind the power curve.

*   **Set Clear Priorities:** Use the fundraising tool to focus all members on the Central Power Hub until you reach Level 4. Unlocking the Weapon Crafting Station is the single biggest power spike a clan can provide.
*   **Prune Inactive Members:** With an initial cap of 30 members, every slot is valuable. Monitor the "Last Online" status in the member list and remove players who haven't contributed to the weekly project.
*   **Coordinate Nest Runs:** Schedule specific times for members to meet at the base and turn in clues together. The time saved by having a smaller search radius allows your team to clear more nests in a single session.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I belong to more than one clan at a time?**

A: No, your character can only be a member of one clan at a time. If you wish to join a new one, you must leave your current clan, which may result in a short cooldown period before you can join another.

**Q: Do I lose my Clan Credits if I leave my clan?**

A: No, Clan Credits are a personal currency tied to your character. However, you will lose access to the Clan Vendor and other facilities until you join a new clan that has those facilities unlocked.

**Q: What is the fastest way to level up a clan in The Division Resurgence?**

A: The fastest method is to have a full squad of four clan members consistently running the Enemy Nest weekly projects. Submitting four clues at once provides the most efficient supply-to-time ratio, as detailed in this **the division resurgence clans guide**.

**Q: Are there exclusive weapons only found in the Clan Vendor?**

A: While most weapons can be found in the open world, the Clan Vendor often sells specific High-End blueprints and Augmentation Modules that are extremely rare elsewhere. These are essential for endgame min-maxing.
