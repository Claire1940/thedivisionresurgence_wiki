## Essential First Steps: Servers and Settings

One of the most critical decisions you will make happens before you even fire a bullet. Upon logging in, you must select between American, European, or Asian servers. It is vital to understand that these servers are completely isolated from one another. While the game supports full crossplay between iOS, Android, and PC emulators, you cannot group with friends who are on a different regional server.

> **💡 Tip:** Even if you aren't in Europe, many players found the European servers to be highly stable during testing. If your clan is based there, don't be afraid to join them; input lag is generally negligible.

Once you are in the game, head straight to your settings. By default, the game often caps performance at 30fps to save battery. However, for a competitive shooter, this can feel "choppy." If your device supports it, switch to 45 or 60fps immediately. While this may slightly reduce your graphical fidelity, the increased responsiveness is a massive advantage in both PvP and high-stakes PvE encounters.

## Choosing and Mastering Specializations

Specializations in Resurgence function as your character class. Unlike some RPGs where you are locked into a choice, this game is quite flexible. You can visit the coordinator at the Base of Operations early in the campaign to swap between them.

| Specialization | Primary Focus | Best For |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Vanguard** | Direct Firepower | Solo players and DPS enthusiasts |
| **Bulwark** | Tanking/Shields | Frontline players who enjoy high survivability |
| **Field Medic** | Support/Healing | Team players focused on group sustainability |
| **Demolitionist** | Area of Effect | Clearing large groups of enemies quickly |

Each specialization has a dedicated guide within the menu that provides objectives to help you learn the class mechanics. Completing these objectives rewards you with specialization points, which are essential for filling out your skill tree.

## Understanding Gear Level and Power Score

A major departure from previous Division games is how gear levels function. In Resurgence, gear has an independent level that is separate from your character level. This means if you find a high-end (yellow) backpack with a perfect talent at level 6, you can continue to level it up all the way to level 40.

**Power Score** is the metric used to determine if you are ready for specific missions. It isn't just about the level of your gun; it is influenced by:
*   The level of your gear and weapons.
*   Mods equipped on your equipment.
*   Account-wide achievements and unlocks.
*   OS Protocols (your watch upgrades).

> **⚠️ Warning:** When dismantling gear that you have upgraded, you only receive 90% of the materials back. For OS Protocols, this return drops to 80%. Be selective about what you choose to level up in the early game.

## Combat Mechanics: Damage Types

As you progress into the late teens and level 20s, you will notice colored icons on your weapons. These represent damage types, a system that becomes mandatory for success against late-game bosses.

| Damage Type | Color Icon | Primary Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Blast** | Red | High impact and explosive damage |
| **Shredding** | Light Blue | Effective against specific armor types |
| **Piercing** | Dark Blue | Bypassing heavy defensive shields |

Matching the damage type of your weapon to the shield type of a boss is essential in 2026. Always try to keep at least one weapon of each type in your inventory so you can swap on the fly during missions.

## Managing the Daily XP Cap and Progression

The Division Resurgence utilizes a daily XP system to pace progression. On your character screen, look for the orange arrow icon. When this is lit, you are receiving full XP. Once it turns gray, your XP gains are significantly reduced. This doesn't stop you from playing, but it does mean you should prioritize your activities wisely while the bonus is active.

### Daily Activity Priority List
1.  **Daily Quests:** These are the fastest way to earn XP and Phoenix Credits. You get five free rerolls per day to swap out objectives you don't like.
2.  **Main Campaign:** These provide the largest chunks of XP and unlock new world areas.
3.  **Hostile Nests:** These are instanced missions that rotate every 15 minutes. Look for "Heroic" nests that stay active for 24 hours for the best farming.
4.  **Open World Activities:** You can receive rewards from up to 10 of these per day. Use them to target specific upgrade materials.
5.  **Dark Zone:** If you are comfortable with PvPvE, the Dark Zone offers high XP and some of the best loot in the game.

## Crafting and Inventory Management

Crafting in Resurgence is a slow process and is often gated by timers. A standard item can take up to 24 hours to craft. Because of the heavy RNG (random number generation) involved in crafting high-end gear, it is generally recommended to rely on loot drops from missions and the Dark Zone rather than spending all your materials at the workbench.

Instead of selling unneeded gear, you should almost always **deconstruct** it. Materials for upgrading your existing "God Roll" items are far more valuable than the credits you receive from vendors. Credits are earned easily through daily logins and achievements, whereas high-end upgrade materials are rare.

## Navigation and Secrets

While exploring New York, follow the "exclamation points" in your menus. The UI can be overwhelming with sub-menus, but these markers usually lead to free rewards, such as Phoenix Credits or Milestone Caches.

You will also encounter locked boxes requiring a combination. Do not ignore these! The codes are never far away. Check nearby walls, scribbled notes on desks, or look into your "Isaac Database" under the **Cryptic Clues** tab. These boxes often contain superior gear that can give you a significant power boost during the leveling process.

## Monetization and Value

As a free-to-play title, Resurgence offers various microtransactions. While you can play the entire game without spending a dime, some offers provide better value than others if you choose to support the game.

*   **Classified Ops Pass (Battle Pass):** Highly recommended for the "Warlord" exotic assault rifle, which can be leveled up with you and carries you through the majority of the campaign.
*   **Warden Field Supplies:** A monthly subscription that provides daily Phoenix Credits and, more importantly, reduces crafting time by 40% and increases bag space.
*   **Avoid:** Random "Sealed Caches" in the store often have poor odds. It is better to spend your currency on guaranteed materials or the Battle Pass.

> **💡 Tip:** Any purchase you make is locked to the specific character you bought it on. Resurgence is a very single-character focused game, so make sure you are committed to your agent before buying cosmetics or passes.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I play with friends on different servers in the division resurgence guide?**

A: No. While the game features cross-platform play between mobile and PC emulators, characters created on the American, European, or Asian servers are isolated. You must all be on the same server to group up.

**Q: What happens when I hit the daily XP cap?**

A: Your XP gains will be significantly reduced (the orange arrow will turn gray). You can still play and earn loot, but your character leveling will slow down until the daily reset.

**Q: Is crafting the best way to get high-end gear?**

A: Generally, no. Crafting involves long timers and high RNG. It is better to farm Nests or the Dark Zone for gear drops and use your crafting materials to upgrade the "Improve" stats of the gear you already have.

**Q: How do I unlock the Warlord exotic weapon?**

A: The most reliable way to unlock the Warlord early in 2026 is through the Classified Ops Pass. This weapon is a returning favorite from the original game and is excellent for clearing campaign content.
