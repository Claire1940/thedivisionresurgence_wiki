## Understanding PvP Game Modes

The Division Resurgence offers two primary ways to engage with other players. While both require tactical awareness, they offer vastly different experiences ranging from structured competitive play to high-stakes survival.

### PvP Domination (4v4)
Domination is a fast-paced, objective-based mode where two teams of four compete to capture and hold three strategic points: A, B, and C. Matches typically last 10 minutes, and the goal is to reach a specific point threshold or have the highest score when the timer expires.

*   **Flag B Priority:** Located in the center of the map, Flag B is the most contested area. Controlling this point often dictates the flow of the match.
*   **Capture Mechanics:** Agents must stand within the designated capture zone. The more teammates present, the faster the capture progresses.
*   **Map Knowledge:** Because these maps are smaller than the open-world NYC map, flanking routes and cover placement are critical.

### The Dark Zone (PvPvE)
The Dark Zone (DZ) is a lawless area where the rules of engagement are dictated by players. It is a "survival" style experience where you face powerful NPCs and other agents simultaneously.

## Dark Zone Survival Strategies

The Dark Zone is the most rewarding area in the game, but it is also the most dangerous. To succeed, you must manage your infection levels and extraction timing perfectly.

### Navigating Contaminated Areas
As you explore, your infection meter will slowly fill. If it reaches 100%, your health will deplete rapidly. You can reset this meter by visiting a Safe House. Safe Houses also offer unique services:
1.  **Smuggling Ring:** Pay 300 DZ credits to safely smuggle 3 items out without an extraction.
2.  **Intelligence:** Use credits to reveal the positions of all other agents on the map for 30 seconds.
3.  **Hacked Terminals:** Interact with these to clear your Rogue status if you wish to return to being a neutral agent.

### The Extraction Process
Extracting loot is the most vulnerable moment for any agent. When you call for an extraction, every player in the vicinity is alerted.

1.  **Call the Helicopter:** Interact with the extraction point and wait for the one-minute timer.
2.  **Deploy Defenses:** You can spend DZ currency to activate **Sentry Turrets** at the extraction site to help fend off NPCs and Rogues.
3.  **The Second Rope:** In The Division Resurgence, the extraction helicopter drops two ropes. One is for your loot bag, and the second allows you to **physically extract** from the DZ entirely without walking back to a checkpoint.

## Economy and Gear Progression

PvP success is heavily tied to your gear. The Dark Zone features a unique currency and vendor system that allows you to target specific upgrades.

### Dark Zone Credits
You can earn up to **10,000 DZ credits per week**. These are obtained by completing landmarks, killing bosses, and looting boxes. You can also deconstruct DZ mods to earn small amounts of currency that do not count toward your weekly cap.

### The Dark Zone Vendor
Located in the Base of Operations, the DZ vendor sells high-end gear that rotates daily.
*   **Targeted Loot:** You can set "Targeted Loot" for mods to ensure you are farming the specific set bonuses your build requires.
*   **Recalibration Books:** Look for books for exotic weapons like the **Lady Death**. While these can drop from DZ bosses, the vendor is a reliable backup.
*   **Exotic Enhancement Kits:** These are rare items used to upgrade the tier of your exotic weapons. They often have a lifetime purchase limit, so buy them whenever they are available.

## Tactical Tips for Success

*   **Environmental Hazards:** Always look for red barrels. Shooting these causes explosions that deal significantly more damage than standard gunfire, which is perfect for clearing groups of enemies at a landmark.
*   **Weapon Categories:** When comparing gear, only compare weapons within the same category (e.g., SMG vs. SMG). A Sniper will always show higher base damage, but its lower fire rate makes it a poor choice for close-quarters PvP.
*   **Group Play:** While solo play is possible, the Dark Zone is balanced for four-man groups. Use the [official Ubisoft Division Resurgence site](https://www.ubisoft.com/en-us/game/the-division/resurgence) to find community Discord servers for matchmaking.
*   **Loot Everything:** Even if a weapon is "worse" than your current one, pick it up. Dismantling junk gear provides the materials needed to craft and upgrade your primary loadout.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Never trust a random player in the Dark Zone. Even if they help you clear a landmark, they can turn Rogue the moment the extraction rope drops to steal your hard-earned loot.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I increase my weekly Dark Zone credit limit?**

A: The standard limit is fixed at 10,000 credits per week. However, you can bypass this slightly by deconstructing Dark Zone-specific mods, which grants currency that does not count against the 10,000-credit cap.

**Q: What happens if I die while Rogue in the Dark Zone?**

A: If you die as a Rogue, you will lose a significant amount of Dark Zone XP and drop all your contaminated loot. Other players who kill you will receive rewards for "neutralizing" a Rogue agent.

**Q: Is there a specific "the division resurgence pvp guide" for weapon talents?**

A: Weapon talents are randomized and rotate daily at the Dark Zone vendor. For PvP, look for talents like "Steady Hands" (for recoil control) or "Rapid Charge" (for faster skill deployment) to gain an edge over other players.

**Q: Can I play PvP Domination solo?**

A: Yes, you can queue solo, and the game will match you with three other teammates. However, coordinated teams using voice chat will almost always have an advantage when capturing the B flag.
