### The Smuggling Ring "Glitch"
If you find yourself trapped by rogue agents and unable to reach an extraction point, you can utilize the Smuggling Ring. Located inside DZ safe houses, this mechanic allows you to bypass the traditional extraction "rope" entirely.

*   **Cost:** 300 DZ Credits
*   **Capacity:** 3 items per transaction

If it hits 100%, you will die almost instantly. You can reset this by visiting a safe house or simply dying and respawning, though the latter will cause you to drop your loot.

## Optimizing Performance and Fixing Input Glitches

Playing a high-fidelity shooter on mobile often leads to performance-related glitches. Players have reported that mouse input on emulators can feel "wonky" or unresponsive compared to native mobile touch controls. To mitigate these issues and ensure you aren't fighting a **the division resurgence glitch** during a firefight, follow these optimization steps:

1.  **Frame Rate Priority:** Set your graphics to "Performance Mode" in the settings menu. This reduces visual fidelity but stabilizes the frame rate, which is crucial in the Dark Zone.
2.  **Ammo Management:** Unlike the main missions, the Dark Zone does not always restock your ammo automatically upon entry. Always check ammo boxes at landmarks to avoid the "empty clip" glitch in the middle of a boss fight.
3.  **Emulator Calibration:** If you are using an emulator, ensure your keymapping is set to "Smart Mode" to avoid the common cursor-lock glitch that prevents you from turning your character.

> **💡 Tip:** Use the "Second Rope" mechanic during extraction. After the initial one-minute wait, a second rope appears that allows you to extract and exit the Dark Zone simultaneously, saving you a trip back to the checkpoint.

## Advanced Farming: The Targeted Loot System

While some look for a **the division resurgence glitch** to farm specific items, the game actually has a built-in "Targeted Loot" system for mods. By navigating to your activity playlist, you can select specific mod sets (2-set or 3-set bonuses) to focus on. This significantly increases the drop rate for the items you actually need for your specialization.

*   **Exotic Enhancement Kits:** These are limited-time purchases from the DZ vendor. They are essential for upgrading the tier of weapons like the Lady Death or Warlord.
*   **Recalibration Books:** Don't waste your DZ credits buying these from the vendor. They drop frequently from heroic bosses located in the center of the DZ map.

## FAQ

**Q: Is there a working the division resurgence glitch for unlimited credits?**

A: No. Any "glitch" claiming to give unlimited credits is likely a scam or a bannable exploit. The most efficient legitimate way to earn extra credits is to deconstruct Dark Zone mods, which provides a small amount of currency that does not count toward your 10,000 weekly cap.

**Q: Why did my loot disappear after I died in the Dark Zone?**

A: This isn't a glitch; it is a core mechanic. When you die, your contaminated loot drops to the floor. If another player picks it up, the loot is randomized for them to prevent "real money trading" or gear boosting between high and low-level players.

**Q: How do I fix the "Wonky Mouse" glitch when playing on PC?**

A: This usually occurs when using an emulator. Ensure your polling rate is set to 500Hz or lower and that you are using the latest version of your emulation software. Switching between "DirectX" and "Vulkan" rendering modes in the emulator settings can also resolve input lag.

**Q: Can I use safe codes more than once?**

A: No, safes are one-time rewards per character. Once you input the correct code and claim the superior items, the safe will remain open for the remainder of your playthrough.
