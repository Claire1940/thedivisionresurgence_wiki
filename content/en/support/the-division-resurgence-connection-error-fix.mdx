## Understanding Common Connection Errors

Before diving into the solutions, it is important to identify exactly what is going wrong. The Division Resurgence typically presents connectivity issues in three specific ways. Knowing which one you are facing helps narrow down the correct fix.

| Error Symptom | Common Cause | Primary Solution |
|---|---|---|
| **Error Code 60000002** | Handshake failure between device and server | Switch network type (Wi-Fi to Data) |
| **Stuck at 99% Update** | Corrupted patch files or interrupted download | Clear app cache and restart |

## Immediate Network Troubleshooting Steps

The most frequent cause of a network failure in The Division Resurgence is a "soft lock" on your current internet connection. This happens when the game's security protocols detect a fluctuation in your IP address or packet loss.

### 1. Toggle Between Wi-Fi and Mobile Data
Surprisingly, many players find that their home Wi-Fi—while stable for streaming—may have firewall settings that interfere with the game. If you are on Wi-Fi and receive a network error, immediately switch to your cellular mobile data. Conversely, if your mobile data is struggling, find a stable Wi-Fi 6 connection.

### 2. The "Retry" Loop Fix
When the game displays a "Retry" button after a connection failure, simply tapping it repeatedly often yields no results. Instead, try this sequence:
1. Tap the **Back** button on your device to return to the main splash screen.
2. Disconnect and reconnect your internet while the game is still in the background.
3. Re-select your region and tap **Confirm**.

### 3. Background Application Refresh
The Division Resurgence is resource-heavy. If you have multiple social media or streaming apps running in the background, they may be "stealing" the bandwidth required for the initial server handshake. Close all recent applications before launching the game to ensure it has priority access to your network hardware.

## Fixing the 99% Program Update Loop

One of the most specific issues players face is getting stuck at the very end of a patch cycle. This 99% hang usually indicates that the game has downloaded the assets but is failing to verify them against the server's manifest.

To resolve this, you must perform a cache refresh. This does not delete your game assets but forces the app to re-verify the existing files.

**How to Clear Cache on Android:**
1. Open your device **Settings**.
2. Navigate to **Apps** or **App Management**.
3. Locate **The Division Resurgence** in the list.
4. Tap on **Storage**.
5. Select **Clear Cache** (Do NOT tap Clear Data).
6. Restart your phone and relaunch the game.

## Server and Regional Connectivity Adjustments

Sometimes the problem isn't your phone or your internet, but the specific Ubisoft server you are trying to reach. If the Asia server is undergoing maintenance or facing a DDoS attack, you will receive a connection error regardless of your local signal strength.

If you are stuck on the loading screen, look for the server selection option (usually a globe icon or text indicating your current region). Attempt to switch from your default region to another. For example, if you are in North America, try connecting to the Europe server. While your "ping" (latency) might be slightly higher, this is an effective **the division resurgence connection error fix** for getting past login blocks. Once you have successfully logged in once, you can often switch back to your home region without further issues.

> **Tip:** If the game doesn't show the server list because of the error, try using a VPN set to a different country to force the game to offer a different regional server.

## Advanced Technical Fixes: VPNs and DNS

If standard troubleshooting fails, the issue may lie with your ISP (Internet Service Provider) blocking the specific ports used by Ubisoft. This is common in university dorms, offices, or certain regions with strict internet filtering.

### Using a VPN
A Virtual Private Network (VPN) can bypass local ISP throttling. By routing your traffic through a different server, you can often "clean" the connection path. Many players have reported that connecting to a VPN server located in a region where the game is officially supported (like certain European or Asian territories) immediately resolves the "Network Failure" message.

### Changing Your DNS
Sometimes your default DNS cannot resolve the game's update addresses. Switching to a public DNS can improve connectivity:
*   **Google DNS:** 8.8.8.8 and 8.8.4.4
*   **Cloudflare DNS:** 1.1.1.1

## Troubleshooting Checklist for 2026

If you are still struggling, work through this checklist in order. Most players find success within the first three steps.

1. **Restart the Device**: Clears temporary RAM glitches that might affect the network card.
2. **Check Official Status**: Visit the [Official Ubisoft Help Center](https://www.ubisoft.com/en-us/help) to see if servers are down for maintenance.
3. **Toggle Airplane Mode**: This forces your mobile device to search for the nearest cell tower, often refreshing a stale data connection.
4. **Update the App**: Ensure you aren't running an outdated version from an APK site; always use the official Play Store or App Store versions.
5. **Storage Space**: Ensure you have at least 5GB of free space. The game needs "breathing room" to unpack temporary files during the connection phase.

## FAQ

**Q: Why does my game get stuck at 99% during the program update?**

A: This is usually a verification error. The game has the files but cannot confirm them with the server. Using the **the division resurgence connection error fix** of clearing your app cache and switching from Wi-Fi to mobile data usually resolves this instantly.

**Q: Will clearing my cache delete my character?**

A: No. Clearing the cache only removes temporary files used to speed up the app. Your character data is stored on Ubisoft's servers. However, do not click "Clear Data" or "Uninstall" unless your account is properly linked to an external ID.

**Q: Is Error Code 60000002 a permanent ban?**

A: No, Error Code 60000002 is strictly a network communication failure. It means your device cannot establish a secure handshake with the login server. It is not related to account standing or bans.

**Q: Can I play The Division Resurgence offline?**

A: No. As a live-service persistent world game, The Division Resurgence requires a constant active internet connection for all gameplay modes, including solo missions.
