## Getting Started: Servers and Specializations

Your journey begins with a critical choice that cannot be easily undone: your server region. The Division Resurgence offers American, European, and Asian servers. While the game features full cross-play between iOS and Android (and even those using PC emulators), these servers exist in complete isolation. If you intend to play with friends or join a specific clan, you must ensure everyone selects the same region, as you cannot group across different servers.

Once you complete the tutorial, you will select a Specialization. In Resurgence, these function like character classes, offering unique skills and signature weapons. Do not feel paralyzed by this choice; you can visit the Coordinator at the Base of Operations early in the campaign to switch your Specialization or reallocate your skill points.

| Feature | Details |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Server Regions** | **American, European, Asian** (Isolated from each other) |
| **Cross-play** | Fully supported between **iOS and Android** |
| **Specializations** | Can be **swapped and respec'd** at the Base of Operations |
| **Graphics** | Supports **30, 45, and 60 FPS** (Device dependent) |

> **💡 Tip:** If the game feels "choppy," check your settings. The game often defaults to 30 FPS, but switching to 60 FPS provides a much smoother experience, even if it slightly lowers graphical fidelity.

## Understanding Gear and Power Score

One of the biggest departures from *The Division 2* is how gear levels function. In previous games, gear dropped at a fixed level. In Resurgence, your gear has an independent level separate from your character level. This means if you find a high-end backpack with perfect stats at level 10, you can continue to level that specific item up all the way to level 40.

Your overall combat effectiveness is measured by **Power Score**. This is a cumulative value that represents your account's total progression. It is influenced by:
*   The level and rarity of your equipped weapons and gear.
*   The level of mods slotted into your equipment.
*   Account-wide unlocks, such as achievements and career milestones.

| Metric | Function |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Character Level** | Unlocks new story missions and skills. |
| **Gear Level** | The individual strength of a specific item; **can be upgraded**. |
| **Power Score** | Determines your readiness for **Challenging and Heroic** content. |

## The Division Resurgence FAQ: Combat and Damage Types

As you progress into the mid-to-late game (levels 20+), you will notice that enemies begin to utilize specific shield types. To counter these effectively, you must pay attention to the **Damage Types** assigned to your weapons. Every weapon in the game is categorized by a colored icon representing its damage specialty.

Matching the correct damage type to an enemy's shield is not optional in high-level content; it is a requirement for success. Always try to carry one weapon of each type in your inventory to swap as needed.

## Maximizing Daily XP and Progression

To prevent player burnout and maintain a balanced economy, the game implements a **Daily XP Boost/Cap**. You can track this via the orange arrow icon near your character level. 

*   **Orange Arrow:** You are receiving full XP gains for all activities.
*   **Gray Arrow:** You have reached your daily limit, and XP gains are significantly reduced.

While you can still play when the arrow is gray, it is much more efficient to focus on non-XP activities like gear sorting or Dark Zone farming once the cap is hit. Additionally, you can only receive rewards from **10 open-world activities** per daily reset.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Side missions do not scale with your level. The XP reward is locked to the level at which you received the mission. It is best to complete them as they appear rather than saving them for later.

## Crafting and Economy Management

Crafting in Resurgence is a secondary system rather than a primary source of gear. Unlike the instant crafting in previous titles, Resurgence uses a **time-based system**. Even common items can take up to 24 hours to craft. Because of the high RNG involved in crafting rolls, it is generally recommended to save your materials for upgrading your existing high-end gear rather than gambling on new crafts.

When you find yourself with a full inventory of unneeded gear, you have two choices: sell or dismantle.
1.  **Dismantle:** This is the most recommended path. Materials are rare and essential for leveling up your "forever" gear.
2.  **Dismantling Warning:** You only receive **90% of the materials** you previously invested into an item when you dismantle it. For OS Protocols, this return drops to **80%**.

## Monetization and Value for Money

While the game is fully playable as a free-to-play (F2P) title, there are several microtransactions available. If you are looking to spend money, the community generally agrees on two specific purchases that offer the highest value:

1.  **Classified Ops Pass (Battle Pass):** This provides a steady stream of materials and usually includes an exotic weapon, such as the *Warlord* assault rifle, which can carry you through the entire campaign.
2.  **Warden Field Supplies:** A monthly subscription that grants daily Phoenix Credits, 30 extra inventory slots, and a 40% reduction in crafting time.

Please note that purchases are **character-specific**. Unlike the account-wide unlocks in *The Division 2*, items bought on one character in Resurgence will not transfer to your alt characters.

## FAQ

**Q: Is there a way to increase the daily XP cap in The Division Resurgence?**

A: No, the daily XP boost is a fixed threshold that resets every 24 hours. However, you can maximize your efficiency by prioritizing Main Missions and Daily Quests while the orange arrow is active, saving lower-XP activities for after the cap is reached.

**Q: Can I play with friends on different servers?**

A: No. While the game supports cross-play between devices, the American, European, and Asian servers are completely isolated. You must be on the same server to see, group with, or join a clan with your friends.

**Q: What happens if I dismantle a weapon I have already upgraded?**

A: You will get back 90% of the materials you invested into that weapon. While you lose a small amount (10%), it allows you to transfer the majority of your progress from an old weapon to a new, better one.

**Q: Where can I find the codes for the locked loot boxes in the open world?**

A: Codes are usually hidden in the environment nearby—look for scribbles on walls or notes on desks. Alternatively, check your Database menu under the "Cryptic Clues" tab; collectibles found in the area often contain the combinations needed to unlock local boxes.
