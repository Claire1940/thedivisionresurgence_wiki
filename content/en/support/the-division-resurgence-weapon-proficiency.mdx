## Understanding the Weapon Proficiency System

In The Division Resurgence, weapons are tied to blueprints. When you find a new weapon in the open world or as a mission reward, you automatically unlock its corresponding blueprint at the Base of Operations. Proficiency represents your familiarity and "expertise" with that specific weapon model. 

As you use a weapon to neutralize enemies, you gain proficiency points. These points allow you to upgrade the blueprint itself. This is a massive shift from traditional Division gameplay because it means that even if you find a "Superior" (purple) or "High-End" (gold) version of a gun, its base stats are heavily influenced by how much work you have put into that weapon's proficiency track.

### Proficiency Level Requirements
To advance your proficiency, you must complete specific combat challenges while the weapon is equipped. A common requirement is neutralizing a set number of enemies who are within five levels of your current character level. This prevents players from "power-leveling" by farming low-level starter zones.

| Proficiency Level | Common Requirement | Primary Reward |
|---|---|---|
| **Level 1** | Neutralize 30 Enemies | +5% Accuracy |
| **Level 3** | Neutralize 100 Enemies | +5% Vertical Stability |
| **Level 5** | Neutralize 250 Enemies | +5% Horizontal Stability |
| **Level 10** | Neutralize 750+ Enemies | Max Blueprint Stat Buffs |

> **💡 Tip:** Always check your weapon's proficiency tab in the Armory. If you are close to a milestone, it is often worth finishing the grind before crafting a new version of that gun to ensure the highest possible base stats.

## Tactical Augmentations: Pushing Blueprints Further

While proficiency levels provide stability and handling buffs, Tactical Augmentations are the key to raw damage output. These are rare resources found through high-level play, such as Challenging Nests or Dark Zone extractions. Once found, these augments are "invested" into a specific weapon blueprint.

Unlike proficiency, which is earned through usage, Tactical Augmentations are a currency-based upgrade. They provide massive percentage-based boosts to multi-shot damage and overall weapon power.

### Augmentation Progression Table
Investing in your favorite weapon's blueprint follows a tiered structure. Each stage requires more rare augments but yields significantly higher returns.

| Augmentation Tier | Resource Cost | Bonus Granted |
|---|---|---|
| **Stage 1** | 1 Augment | +10% Multi-shot Damage |
| **Stage 2** | 2 Augments | +20% Weapon Damage |
| **Stage 4** | 4 Augments | +60% Total Damage Output |
| **Stage 5 (Max)** | 5+ Augments | +80% Total Damage Output |

## Crafting Expertise and the 24-Hour Rule

The Division Resurgence weapon proficiency is only half of the equation; the other half is Crafting Expertise. Every time you craft an item at the Base of Operations, you gain Expertise XP. 

At low Expertise levels, your chance of crafting a High-End item is virtually zero. However, as you reach Expertise Level 10, the odds shift dramatically. This creates a gameplay loop where you should craft items constantly—even if you don't need them—simply to level up your workbench.

*   **The Timer:** Most gear and weapon crafting takes **24 hours** to complete.
*   **The Strategy:** Use your daily crafting slot every single day. If you have the Warden Field Supplies (monthly pass), this time is reduced by 40%.
*   **The Materials:** Deconstructing unneeded gear returns 90% of the invested materials. Always deconstruct rather than sell to keep your crafting loop active.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not spend rare materials on "Tier 0" blueprints once you reach level 30. Save your high-end resources for Tier 1 and Tier 2 blueprints, which offer significantly higher base power scores.

## Recalibration: Fine-Tuning Your God-Rolls

Once you reach Level 20, the Equipment Improvement Station unlocks, allowing for Recalibration. This is where you take a weapon that has high proficiency and "fix" its suboptimal rolls.

In Resurgence, you can only recalibrate **one** attribute or talent on a piece of gear or a weapon. Once you choose which slot to change, all other slots are locked forever. This makes the search for "near-perfect" loot essential. You want a gun that has three out of four desired stats, allowing you to use Recalibration to swap the fourth stat for something like Critical Hit Damage or Armor Piercing.

| Item Quality | Recalibration Possible? | Required Materials |
|---|---|---|
| **Superior (Blue)** | No | N/A |
| **High-End (Gold)** | **Yes** | Recalibration Credits + Materials |
| **Exotic (Orange)** | **Yes** | Exotic Components |

## Damage Types and Strategic Swapping

As you progress into the 2026 endgame, simply having high proficiency isn't enough. You must match your weapon's damage type to the enemy's shield color. Weapons are categorized into three types:

1.  **Blast Damage (Red):** Effective against heavy armor and grouped enemies.
2.  **Shredding Damage (Light Blue):** Designed to strip away standard kinetic shields.
3.  **Piercing Damage (Dark Blue):** Best for penetrating high-tech tactical plating.

By maintaining high **the division resurgence weapon proficiency** across at least one weapon of each damage type, you ensure that you are never "hard-stuck" against a specific boss encounter.

## Power Score vs. Character Level

It is important to distinguish between your character level and your Power Score. Your character level unlocks new story missions and specializations, but your Power Score—heavily influenced by weapon proficiency and gear levels—determines your actual survivability.

You can take a Level 1 weapon and "Improve" it all the way to Level 40 using standard materials. This means if you find an Exotic like the **Warlord** early in the campaign, you can keep it relevant for the entire journey by consistently upgrading its level and working on its proficiency track.

> **💡 Tip:** Power Score is also boosted by "Account Achievements." Don't ignore the exclamation points in your menus; many of those small rewards provide permanent, account-wide stat bumps.

## FAQ

**Q: Does weapon proficiency carry over to new characters?**

A: No. The Division Resurgence is a single-character focused title. Proficiency, blueprints, and microtransactions are currently locked to the specific character they were earned or purchased on.

**Q: What is the fastest way to farm weapon proficiency?**

A: The fastest method is farming "Nests" or Open World Activities. Ensure the enemies you are killing are not more than five levels below you, or you will receive zero progress toward your proficiency goals.

**Q: Can I recalibrate an Exotic weapon?**

A: Yes, Exotics can be recalibrated at the Equipment Improvement Station starting at level 20. However, they require rare Exotic Components which are much harder to obtain than standard recalibration materials.

**Q: Why is my crafting taking 24 hours?**

A: This is a standard mechanic in The Division Resurgence to encourage daily logins. You can reduce this timer by purchasing the Warden Field Supplies or by reaching higher tiers of the Classified Ops Pass.
