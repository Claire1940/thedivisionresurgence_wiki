## Initial Setup and Performance Optimization

Before diving into specific gameplay glitches, it is vital to ensure your hardware meets the necessary thresholds. Many "bugs" reported by players are often performance bottlenecks caused by insufficient storage or improper settings. The game requires a significant footprint on your device, and failing to meet these can lead to asset-loading failures and "ghosting" textures.

| Requirement | Minimum Specification | Recommended Specification |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Storage Space** | 10.5 GB (Base) | **17 GB+** (Total) |
| **Frame Rate** | 30 FPS (Default) | **60 FPS** (High Performance) |
| **Connectivity** | 4G/LTE | **Wi-Fi 6 or 5G** |
| **Account** | Guest Account | **Ubisoft Connect** (Linked) |

> **💡 Tip:** If the game feels "choppy," check your settings menu immediately. The game defaults to 30 FPS to save battery, but most modern devices support 45 or 60 FPS. Toggling this can resolve perceived input lag that many players mistake for a server-side bug.

## Current The Division Resurgence Known Bugs List & Technical Faults

The following issues have been identified by the community and the "Warden" field support teams. While some are slated for hotfixes in the coming weeks, others require manual intervention from the player.

### 1. Server Isolation and Cross-Play Confusion
A common point of confusion—often mistaken for a social bug—is the inability to find friends in-game. The Division Resurgence features full cross-play between iOS, Android, and PC emulators. However, characters are locked to the specific region selected at the start: American, European, or Asian servers. If your squad cannot see each other, it is likely due to server isolation rather than a software glitch.

### 2. The "Delta" Error Loop
Connectivity remains the most cited entry on **the division resurgence known bugs list**. Delta-03 and Delta-04 errors typically signify a handshake failure between your device and the Ubisoft servers. This is particularly prevalent when transitioning from cellular data to a Wi-Fi network while the game is running in the background.

### 3. UI Exclamation Point Persistence
Many Agents have reported that notification markers (exclamation points) remain on the menu even after all new items or achievements have been claimed. While annoying, this is a visual-only bug. Following the "exclamation point trail" through sub-menus usually clears them, but in some instances, a full game restart is required to refresh the UI state.

## Gameplay and Progression Blockers

Progression in Resurgence is tied to your Power Score and Character Level. Some players have referred to **the division resurgence known bugs list** for UI-related issues where mission requirements do not appear to update in real-time.

### XP Cap vs. Progression Bug
It is important to distinguish between the daily XP cap and a progression bug. Resurgence utilizes an orange arrow icon near your level to indicate an active XP boost. When this arrow turns gray, your XP gains slow down significantly. This is a design choice to prevent extreme power-leveling and is not a technical fault. 

### Crafting Timers and RNG
The crafting system in Resurgence differs significantly from the PC/Console titles. Every item, even common ones, has a 24-hour base crafting timer. Some players have reported this as a "stuck" progress bar. Ensure you have checked the timer before reporting a bug. Additionally, the high level of RNG involved in the workbench means that "failed" crafts with poor stats are an intended part of the grind, not a malfunction of the crafting station.

| Feature | Intended Mechanic | Common Misconception |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Daily Rewards** | Limit of 10 Open World activities | "Rewards stopped dropping" |
| **Crafting** | 24-Hour Real-Time Wait | "Crafting station is stuck" |
| **Specializations** | Switchable at the Base Coordinator | "Selected the wrong class" |
| **Side Missions** | Static XP (Does not scale) | "XP reward is too low" |

## Dark Zone and PvP Instability

The Dark Zone (DZ) is the most demanding environment in the game, combining high NPC density with real-time player interactions. Consequently, it is where most visual and synchronization issues occur. Keep an eye on **the division resurgence known bugs list** for updates on Delta errors specifically occurring during extraction sequences.

*   **Betrayal Mechanics:** Friendly fire is disabled for party members, but "random" allies can turn Rogue at any time. If you are suddenly taking damage from a non-Rogue player, they have likely initiated the Rogue protocol—this is a core gameplay mechanic, not a bug.
*   **Extraction UI Lag:** Occasionally, the extraction timer may desync by 1–2 seconds. It is recommended to stay within the extraction zone until the "Loot Secured" notification fully clears the screen.
*   **PvP Domination Scoreboard:** There is a known visual bug where the scoreboard kill count may favor the player who lands the finishing blow rather than the one who dealt the majority of the damage.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Never attempt to "force close" the app during a Dark Zone extraction to fix a visual glitch. Doing so will result in the immediate loss of all contaminated loot in your backpack.

## Account Management and Rewards

With the launch of Resurgence, many players are claiming cross-game rewards from [The Division 2 official site](https://www.ubisoft.com/en-us/game/the-division/the-division-2). Issues often appear on **the division resurgence known bugs list** regarding the delivery of these items.

### Reward Delivery Delays
Rewards like the Firefighter Set or JTF Apparel are delivered via the in-game mail system. 
1.  Ensure you are logged into the **same Ubisoft Connect account** used for previous tests or Division 2 play.
2.  Check the "Events" tab and manually claim the "Cross-Rewards."
3.  Navigate to your Stash to retrieve the items.

Note that rewards are character-specific. If you delete a character after claiming a one-time reward, that reward is lost permanently. This is a critical point that the community-driven **the division resurgence known bugs list** helps identify for new players.

## How to Report a Bug

If you encounter an issue not listed here, contributing to **the division resurgence known bugs list** through the official reporter is the best way to ensure a fix.

1.  **Screen Capture:** Take a screenshot or video of the issue as it occurs.
2.  **Note the Error Code:** Delta, Echo, and Mike codes are the most helpful for developers.
3.  **In-Game Support:** Use the "Report a Bug" feature found in the settings menu.
4.  **Community Discord:** Join the official Division Resurgence Discord to see if other Agents are experiencing the same fault.

## FAQ

**Q: Why can't I play with my friends even though we are both on mobile?**

A: You must ensure you are on the same regional server (Americas, Europe, or Asia). Characters cannot currently be transferred between servers, and cross-server grouping is not supported.

**Q: Is the 24-hour crafting timer a bug?**

A: No, this is an intended mechanic in The Division Resurgence. You can reduce this time by purchasing the Warden Field Supplies or upgrading your workbench, but the base timer is intentionally long.

**Q: I found a "lockbox" but I don't have the code. Is this a glitch?**

A: Lockboxes require a code found in the environment. Check nearby walls for scribbled numbers or look in your Database menu under "Cryptic Clues" for the solution.

**Q: Will my progress from the Beta carry over to the 2026 launch?**

A: No. All progression from previous play-tests was reset for the official launch to ensure a level playing field for all new Agents. Only specific cosmetic rewards earned in the Beta will carry over.
