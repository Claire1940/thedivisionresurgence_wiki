### Managing Cache and Storage

A common reason for the **the division resurgence facebook** login to fail is a lack of local storage space. The game requires significant overhead to handle the handshake between the Ubisoft servers and the Facebook API.

1.  Navigate to your phone's **Settings**.
2.  Go to **Apps** or **App Management**.
3.  Select **The Division Resurgence**.
4.  Tap on **Storage & Cache**.
5.  Select **Clear Cache** (Note: Do not select "Clear Data" unless you have already backed up your account, as this will delete your local guest profile).

> **⚠️ Warning:** If you are playing on a "Guest Account," you must link it to a social profile immediately. If you uninstall the game or clear data before linking, your progress may be permanently lost.

## FAQ

**Q: Why can't I link my The Division Resurgence account to Facebook?**

A: This is usually caused by the Facebook app on your phone being logged into a different account than the one you are trying to link, or a conflict in the app's cache. Try logging out of the Facebook app, clearing the game's cache, and then attempting the link again through the in-game settings.

**Q: Will I lose my progress if I change my Facebook password?**

A: No, changing your password will not delete your progress. However, you may be prompted to "Re-authenticate" the next time you launch **the division resurgence facebook** login portal. Simply enter your new credentials to resume your progress.

**Q: Are there exclusive rewards for following The Division Resurgence on Facebook?**

A: Yes, Ubisoft frequently runs "Social Milestones." When the official page reaches a certain number of followers, all players typically receive an in-game mail containing crafting materials, vanity items, or specialized ammo caches.

**Q: My game keeps crashing when I try to open the Facebook community tab in-game. How do I fix this?**

A: This is often a RAM management issue. Ensure no other heavy applications are running in the background. If the problem persists, check for a system update on your device, as older OS versions can struggle with integrated web-viewers used for social menus.
