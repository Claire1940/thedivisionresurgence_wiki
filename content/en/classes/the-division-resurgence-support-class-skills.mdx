## Overview of Support Specializations

In *The Division Resurgence*, "Support" is not limited to just healing. It encompasses crowd control, enemy disruption, and stat-boosting for allies. While every agent can equip basic skills, the true power lies in the Specializations unlocked as you progress through the story. 

Initially, you must choose one specialization at the start of the game. However, once you reach character level 15, the ability to switch between specializations is unlocked, allowing you to level up multiple roles and adapt to your team's needs.

| Specialization | Primary Role | Key Utility | Playstyle |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Field Medic** | Healer / Corrosive DPS | Burst healing and AoE corrosion | Backline Support |
| **Tech Operator** | Combat Engineer | Drone-based disruption and focus fire | Tactical Specialist |
| **Vanguard** | Tactical Leader | Enemy scanning and ally damage buffs | Frontline Support |

> **💡 Tip:** Do not feel locked into your first choice. Reaching level 15 is relatively fast, and having multiple specializations leveled up is required for high-tier challenges like the Lone Wolf mode.

## The Field Medic: Healing and Corrosion

The Field Medic is the backbone of any organized squad. Unlike traditional healers in other RPGs, the Field Medic in *The Division Resurgence* utilizes "Oxidizing" technology. This means they provide massive bursts of healing to allies while simultaneously deploying corrosive substances that melt enemy armor.

### Field Medic Skill Breakdown

The division resurgence support class skills for the Field Medic focus on area-of-effect (AoE) management. By placing a Hive or throwing a Chemical Trap, the Medic can control large portions of the battlefield.

| Skill Name | Type | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Chemical Trap** | Basic Skill | A device that splits into multiple corrosive submines, creating a deadly minefield. |
| **Oxidizing Hive** | Basic Skill | Deploys micro drones that apply corrosion to enemies upon impact. |
| **Oxidizing Swarm** | Signature (Ult) | Releases a massive swarm of drones that target all enemies in range with high corrosion damage. |

The Signature Ability, **Oxidizing Swarm**, is particularly powerful because it can be deployed at the agent's feet for self-defense or aimed at a specific location to clear a room. It charges over time and through dealing damage, making it a frequent tool in your arsenal.

## The Tech Operator: Master of Drones

The Tech Operator is an expert in deploying and controlling combat drones. While they offer less direct healing than the Field Medic, their support comes through "aggro" management and disruption. By forcing enemies to focus on drones rather than players, the Tech Operator provides a "soft" support that keeps the team safe.

### Tech Operator Skill Breakdown

| Skill Name | Type | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Striker Drone** | Basic Skill | Deploys an automated drone that harries enemies and draws fire. |
| **Drone Lock** | Basic Skill | Commands all active drones to focus fire on a single high-priority target. |
| **Drone Overload** | Signature (Ult) | Emits waves that temporarily increase the damage output of all striker drones. |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Drones can be destroyed by enemy EMPs or heavy fire. Always keep an eye on your drone's health bar and be ready to redeploy once the cooldown expires.

## The Vanguard: Tactical Buffs and Scouting

The Vanguard acts as the "tip of the spear." This class is perfect for players who want to support their team while remaining in the thick of the fight. Their version of the division resurgence support class skills emphasizes intelligence gathering and protection.

The **Scan Pulse** is a staple for the Vanguard, highlighting enemies through walls and providing a tactical advantage before a room is even breached. Additionally, the **Commando Smart Cover** provides significant buffs to any ally using that specific cover, increasing their damage resistance and stability.

### Vanguard Signature: Tactical Link
The **Tactical Link** is a callback to the original *Division* title. When activated, it provides a massive boost to the squad's damage and provides aim assist, allowing the team to burn through boss health bars in seconds.

| Skill | Category | Support Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Scan Pulse** | Intel | Identifies enemy positions and weaknesses for the whole team. |
| **Smart Cover** | Fortification | Increases damage and defense for allies using the covered position. |
| **Tactical Link** | Buff | Massive team-wide increase to fire rate and weapon damage. |

## Progression and Skill Trees

Each specialization features a dedicated skill tree. As you play, you earn Specialization XP which grants points to spend on various perks. However, XP is not the only way to max out your character.

### How to Earn Specialization Points

To fully max out a specialization, you need a total of 49 points. These are sourced from two primary locations:

1.  **Specialization XP (30 Points):** Earned simply by playing the game with that specialization active.
2.  **Shade Tech Caches (19 Points):** Found through specific world activities.

| Activity | Reward Type | Importance |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Patrol Enemies** | Rare Drops | Low (Random chance for caches) |
| **World Events** | Shade Tech Caches | **High** (Guaranteed progression) |
| **Lone Wolf Challenge** | Specialization Buffs | **High** (Endgame testing ground) |
| **Value Packs** | Resources | Medium (Optional shop purchases) |

You can reset your skill tree at any point, allowing you to experiment with different paths without penalty. This is vital when switching between solo play and group raids.

## Team Synergy and the Lone Wolf Challenge

While many players focus on their individual power, the true depth of the division resurgence support class skills is revealed in team synergy. A squad consisting of a Bulwark (Tank), a Field Medic (Healer), and a Vanguard (Buffer) is nearly unstoppable in the Dark Zone.

Furthermore, the game features the **Lone Wolf Challenge**, a mode that cycles every 21 days. This mode provides specific buffs to certain specializations. For instance, one cycle might favor the Demolitionist, while the next might buff the Tech Operator. To maximize your rewards in this mode, it is highly recommended to have at least three specializations fully leveled so you can always take advantage of the active buff.

## FAQ

**Q: Which is the best support class for beginners?**

A: The Field Medic is generally considered the best starting support class. Its ability to provide consistent healing makes the early game much more forgiving, and the corrosive damage skills remain relevant even in the endgame.

**Q: Can I use the division resurgence support class skills in solo play?**

A: Absolutely. While these skills are designed for team play, the Field Medic's corrosion and the Tech Operator's drones are incredibly effective at managing enemy aggro when you are playing alone.

**Q: How do I unlock the Tech Operator specialization?**

A: Unlike the starting four classes, the Tech Operator must be unlocked using specific resources earned during your natural playthrough. Most players will unlock it shortly after reaching level 20.

**Q: Does the Vanguard's Pulse work for the whole team?**

A: Yes, any enemy tagged by the Vanguard's Scan Pulse will be visible to all members of the squad, making it one of the most important tactical skills for coordinated group play.
