## The Foundation: Clan Coordination for Raids

You cannot expect to conquer a raid as a lone wolf. In *The Division Resurgence*, joining or leading a high-level Clan is the first step in any serious **the division resurgence raid guide**. Clans unlock at level 10 and provide the infrastructure necessary for end-game success, including specialized crafting stations and passive stat boosts that are vital for surviving raid-tier damage.

As a Clan progresses, the leader can prioritize specific facilities. For raid preparation, focusing on the **Main Power Generator** and the **Gear Crafting Station** is paramount. A higher-level power generator increases the maximum member capacity (starting at 30 and expanding to 34+), allowing for a larger pool of backup players for your raid roster.

### Clan Base Facilities & Raid Benefits

| Facility | Raid Benefit | Upgrade Priority |
|---|---|---|
| **Main Power Generator** | Increases member cap and unlocks other stations. | **High** |
| **Gear Crafting Station** | Provides efficiency bonuses for high-end gear rolls. | **Critical** |
| **Clan Vendor** | Sells superior items and weapon/gear augments. | **Medium** |
| **Weapon Station** | Essential for optimizing DPS output for boss phases. | **High** |

To upgrade these facilities, members must complete "Clue Board" activities. By collecting clues from the Clan Base Intel Officer and clearing hostile nests in New York, players earn resources to donate back to the base. 

> **💡 Tip:** Always run Clan activities in a squad of four. This reduces the search radius for hostile nests and maximizes the resources earned per run, speeding up your access to raid-ready gear.

## Gearing Up: The Dark Zone Pipeline

Before entering a raid, your build needs to be optimized with "Superior" (purple) or "High-End" (gold) equipment. The most efficient way to achieve this is through the Dark Zone. According to our **the division resurgence raid guide**, you should reach level 20 before farming the Dark Zone heavily, as this unlocks **Targeted Loot**. This feature allows you to focus on specific skill mods or gear sets needed for your raid role.

The Dark Zone is a high-risk, high-reward environment where you have 20 minutes to gather contaminated loot and extract it via helicopter. For raid prep, focus on taking down **DZ Leaders** and clearing **Landmarks**. These encounters drop the chipsets and firmware required to push your gear's attributes to the maximum.

### Dark Zone Loot Tiers for Raid Prep

| Activity | Primary Reward | Difficulty |
|---|---|---|
| **DZ Leaders** | Boss-tier gear and high-end chipsets. | **Hard** |
| **Landmarks** | Wave-based rewards and firmware. | **Medium** |
| **Contaminated Zones** | Quick loot chests and crafting materials. | **Easy** |
| **Extraction Defense** | Final chance to secure your raid upgrades. | **Variable** |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Never attempt an extraction with less than two minutes on the clock. Hostile players or "Rogue" agents can cut your extraction rope, and if you don't secure the loot, your time spent farming for the raid is wasted.

## Essential Raid Roles and Specializations

A successful raid team is built on balance. You cannot simply stack four DPS players and expect to survive the complex mechanics found in a **the division resurgence raid guide**. Each player must choose a specialization that complements the team's overall strategy.

### Recommended Raid Roles

1.  **The Tank (Bulwark):** Uses shields and high armor to draw aggro from bosses. Essential for positioning the boss away from fragile teammates.
2.  **The Healer (Combat Medic):** Focuses on Chem Launchers and Hive skills to keep the team's armor repaired. A raid cannot survive without a dedicated healer.
3.  **The Crowd Control (Technician):** Uses turrets, seeker mines, or status effects to manage "adds" (additional enemies) so the DPS can focus on the main objective.
4.  **The DPS (Sharpshooter/Demolitionist):** High-damage builds designed to burn through boss health bars during "damage phases."

## Raid Combat Strategies and Positioning

Once inside the raid, positioning is everything. Most encounters involve specific environmental hazards or "wipe mechanics" that require players to interact with consoles or stand in specific zones.

*   **Communication:** Use in-game voice chat or a dedicated Discord server. Raid mechanics often happen too fast for text chat.
*   **Focus Fire:** When the boss is vulnerable, all DPS players must focus on the same "weak point" to trigger the next phase.
*   **Add Management:** Designated utility players must clear smaller enemies immediately. If "adds" overwhelm the healer, the entire team will fail.
*   **Medkit Management:** Do not waste medkits on minor damage; rely on your healer's skills. Save medkits for "Serious Trauma" alerts when skills are on cooldown.

## Advanced Tactics: Managing Contamination and Filters

In certain raid environments, similar to the Dark Zone, you may encounter contaminated areas that require high-level filters. Ensure your SHD tech is upgraded to provide maximum resistance. If your filter percentage reaches 100%, you will begin taking rapid health damage. In a raid, this usually leads to a wipe because the healer cannot keep up with the environmental damage on top of enemy fire.

Always check your inventory before a raid for:
*   **Ammo Capacity:** Raids are long. Ensure you have high-capacity magazines.
*   **Armor Kits:** Max out your kits at the Base of Operations.
*   **Augments:** Apply the best weapon augments purchased from your Clan Vendor.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the minimum level to start using a the division resurgence raid guide?**

A: While you can join a Clan at level 10, it is highly recommended to be at least level 20-25 with a fully optimized build from the Dark Zone before attempting a raid.

**Q: Do I need a full squad of 4 or 8 for raids?**

A: Raid sizes can vary, but most high-end encounters are designed for coordinated groups of 4 or 8 players. Check the specific raid requirements in the activities menu.

**Q: Can I get raid-tier loot from the Dark Zone?**

A: The Dark Zone provides the "entry-level" high-end gear needed to survive a raid. However, the absolute best "Exotic" and "Raid-Exclusive" sets only drop from completing the raids themselves.

**Q: What happens if I die during a raid boss?**

A: Most raids have limited respawn tokens or "no-respawn" zones. If you die, a teammate must manually revive you. If the entire team dies, you will restart the encounter from the last checkpoint.
