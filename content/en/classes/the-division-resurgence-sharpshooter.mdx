## Tactical Mastery: Cover and Movement

The transition from a recruit to a veteran **the division resurgence sharpshooter** involves mastering the environment. The game’s cover system is not just a suggestion; it is a requirement for survival. Smart agents move from cover to cover to outmaneuver and flank the enemy, never staying in one spot for too long.

### Anticipating Enemy Patterns
Hostile turrets and stationary gunners often fire in predictable windows. You must learn to anticipate these pauses. These brief moments are your opportunity to counterattack or advance to a more advantageous cover. If a hostile gets too close, use your melee "whack" to push them away and create enough space to transition back to your sidearm or reposition.

### Tactical Checklist for Field Agents:
*   **Identify the "High Ground":** Always look for balconies, scaffolding, or trucks that provide a vertical advantage.
*   **Monitor the Flanks:** Use your mini-map and Pulse to ensure no enemies are circling behind you.
*   **Conserve Grenades:** Save your grenades for clustered groups or to disable enemy technology like turrets and drones.
*   **Signature Ready:** Only deploy your signature ability when facing "Yellow Bar" (Elite) enemies or when your team is pinned down.

## Progression and Specialization Trees

As you progress through the 2026 campaign, you will earn specialization points. For **the division resurgence sharpshooter**, these points should be invested into nodes that enhance your role as a glass cannon.

1.  **Marksmanship Tier 1:** Increases base sniper rifle damage by 10%.
2.  **Breath Control:** Reduces weapon sway when aiming through high-magnification scopes.
3.  **Flashbang Grenade Unlock:** Replaces standard grenades with flashbangs, which are superior for stopping aggressive rushers.
4.  **Digital Scope:** Unlocks a specialized optic that highlights enemy weak points in red.

> **Warning:** Do not neglect your armor stats entirely. While your damage is your primary focus, having enough health to survive a single sniper shot from an enemy is necessary for high-level missions.

## FAQ

**Q: Is the Sharpshooter viable for solo play in The Division Resurgence?**

A: Yes, it is viable, but it requires a very cautious playstyle. Solo players should heavily invest in a "Distraction" skill like the Assault Turret to keep enemies occupied while they line up shots.

**Q: What is the best secondary weapon for a Sharpshooter?**

A: Most players find that a high-RPM Submachine Gun (SMG) or a fast-firing Assault Rifle is best for handling enemies that manage to get close to your position.

**Q: How do I get more Signature Ammo for my sniper rifle?**

A: Signature Ammo is earned by performing specific actions tied to your class. For **the division resurgence sharpshooter**, this usually involves landing headshot kills or eliminating elite enemies.

**Q: Can I change my specialization later if I don't like Sharpshooter?**

A: Yes, the game allows you to swap specializations at the base of operations, though you will need to level up each specialization's skill tree independently to maximize their effectiveness in the 2026 endgame.
