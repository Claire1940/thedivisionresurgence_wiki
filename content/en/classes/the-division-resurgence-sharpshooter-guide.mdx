## Understanding the Sharpshooter Specialization

The Sharpshooter is designed for agents who prefer to maintain distance and provide overwatch for their squad. In *The Division Resurgence*, specializations are more than just a label; they define your signature weapon and your role within a tactical unit. While other classes like the Bulwark focus on shields and crowd control, the Sharpshooter excels at raw "Firearms" scaling.

To truly excel, you must focus on your Marksman Rifle (MMR) proficiency. The game utilizes a "Shade Tech" system that blends elements from previous titles, offering a mix of active skills and passive buffs that reward precision. As you progress, you will unlock signature weapons that can turn the tide of a boss encounter with a single well-placed shot.

> **💡 Tip:** Always prioritize "Firearms" (Red) stats on your gear pieces to ensure your Marksman Rifle damage scales effectively against armored elites.

## The Armory and Blueprint System

Before you can dominate from the rooftops, you need the right tools. The Armory is your central hub for weapon and gear progression. Unlike traditional looter-shooters where you rely solely on RNG drops, *Resurgence* allows you to systematically unlock and upgrade weapon blueprints using Proficiency Points.

### Unlocking Marksman Rifle Blueprints
Proficiency Points are the lifeblood of your Sharpshooter's arsenal. These are earned through almost any activity in the game, from main story missions to open-world encounters. In the Armory tab, you can spend these points to unlock specific weapon trees.

| Blueprint Rarity | Unlock Requirement | Expertise Level 5 Benefit |
|---|---|---|
| **Common/Rare** | 2,000 Proficiency Points | Standard Crafting Unlocked |
| **Epic** | Character Level + Previous Tier | Automatic Next Tier Unlock |
| **High-End** | Advanced Proficiency | Max Attribute Potential |

A key mechanic for Sharpshooters is the "linear unlock" system. If you reach Expertise Level 5 with a specific weapon blueprint (like the M44), the game will often automatically unlock the next blueprint in that category. This saves your Proficiency Points for more critical upgrades like Tactical Augmentation.

## Crafting Expertise and Focus

Crafting in 2026 is a time-investment mechanic. When you initiate a craft for a high-end Marksman Rifle, it may take several minutes (typically around six minutes) to complete. However, the quality of the result is heavily dictated by your **Crafting Expertise**.

### Expertise Levels and Rarity Odds
By spending Proficiency Points to level up a specific blueprint's expertise, you increase the probability of rolling a higher-rarity item. For a Sharpshooter, hitting Expertise Level 15 is a major milestone because it unlocks **Crafting Focus**.

| Expertise Level | Common Chance | Rare Chance | Epic Chance | High-End Chance |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Level 1** | 60% | 30% | 10% | 0% |
| **Level 7** | 36% | 36% | 20% | 8% |
| **Level 15+** | 20% | 30% | 35% | 15% |

**Crafting Focus** allows you to weight the attributes of your crafted item. As a Sharpshooter, you should almost always focus on the "Red" (Firearms) attribute. This ensures that your crafted weapon has a significantly higher chance of rolling maximum damage stats rather than stamina or electronics.

## Tactical Augmentation for Snipers

Tactical Augmentation is a specialized resource that allows you to further enhance your blueprints. These items are often rewarded from challenging World Bosses. When applied to a Marksman Rifle blueprint, they provide flat percentage boosts to the weapon's bonus attributes.

*   **Slot 1 Augmentation:** Increases bonus attributes by **30%**.
*   **Slot 2 Augmentation:** Increases bonus attributes by **60%**.

For a Sharpshooter, these augmentations should be prioritized on rifles with high Headshot Damage or Critical Hit Damage bonuses. Because these buffs apply to the *blueprint*, every subsequent weapon you craft from that template will carry those enhanced stats.

## Recalibration Strategies for the Sharpshooter

Once you reach level 18, you unlock the Equipment Improvement Station. This is where you take a "good" rifle and make it "perfect." Recalibration in *The Division Resurgence* follows the strict rule of the franchise: you can only change **one** modifier (attribute or talent) per item. Once you choose which slot to recalibrate, all other slots are locked.

### Recommended Recalibration Priorities
When looking at a Marksman Rifle, you want to hunt for specific talents that reward the Sharpshooter's playstyle.

| Priority | Attribute/Talent | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1** | **Headshot Damage** | Multiplies total damage on precision hits. |
| **Tier 2** | **Critical Hit Damage** | Increases damage when a crit occurs. |
| **Tier 3** | **Underdog Talent** | Grants buffs when firing from cover or at distance. |
| **Tier 4** | **Reload Speed** | Minimizes downtime between sniper shots. |

If you find a high-end rifle with a perfect Headshot Damage roll but a useless utility talent, use your Recalibration Resources to swap that talent for something like "Powerful" (which can restore health based on damage dealt) or "Stand Your Ground" for extra survivability while scoped in.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Recalibration uses a specific limited resource. Do not waste these on Rare or Epic gear; save them for High-End (Gold) items that you plan to use in your final 2026 endgame build.

## Advanced Gear Synergy

While your weapon is your primary tool, your gear sets define your survivability. In *Resurgence*, gear sets take inspiration from both *The Division 1* and *2*. For a Sharpshooter, you are looking for sets that provide "Damage to Targets Out of Cover" or "Marksman Rifle Damage" set bonuses.

1.  **Identify your core stat:** For Sharpshooters, this is always Firearms.
2.  **Match your Gear Focus:** When crafting gear (masks, backpacks, etc.), use the Crafting Focus to ensure you roll Firearms stats.
3.  **Check for Set Bonuses:** Always look for the folder icon on gear blueprints to see if they belong to a set like the "Hunter's Faith" or "Sentry's Call" equivalents in *Resurgence*.

By combining high Expertise levels with Tactical Augmentations and precise Recalibration, you can create a Sharpshooter that can one-shot most standard enemies and provide massive burst damage against bosses in the Dark Zone or high-level incursions.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the best weapon for a Sharpshooter in The Division Resurgence?**

A: While personal preference varies, the M44 and SRS are top-tier choices due to their high base damage and headshot multipliers. Ensure you upgrade their specific blueprints in the Armory to Expertise Level 15 for the best results.

**Q: How do I unlock the Sharpshooter specialization?**

A: Specializations are typically unlocked through main story progression. Once available, you can select it from the character menu. You can switch specializations, but each has its own separate progression tree.

**Q: Can I recalibrate my gear more than once?**

A: You can recalibrate the *same slot* multiple times to try and get a better roll or a different talent, but you cannot recalibrate a *different* slot once the first one has been modified.

**Q: Where do I get Proficiency Points for this the division resurgence sharpshooter guide?**

A: Proficiency Points are earned by completing missions, clearing landmarks in the Dark Zone, and finishing daily projects. They are a universal currency for blueprint progression.
