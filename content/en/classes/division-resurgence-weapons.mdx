## Master the Weapon Categories

With 91 weapons available in the current 2026 meta, Agents have a massive variety of tools at their disposal. Each category serves a specific tactical purpose, and understanding these roles is the first step toward building a cohesive specialization.

### Assault Rifles (AR)
The most versatile **division resurgence weapons**, ARs like the Military 416 and the AK-47 offer a balance of range, damage, and stability. They are the go-to choice for mid-range engagements and general PvE content.

### Submachine Guns (SMG)
SMGs such as the Vector 45 ACP and the MP7 prioritize high rates of fire and critical hit chance. These are best utilized by the Vanguard specialization or players who prefer aggressive, close-range combat.

### Marksman Rifles (MMR) and Rifles
For those who prefer distance, MMRs like the M700 Carbon provide massive single-shot damage. Standard Rifles, such as the Classic M1A, offer a semi-automatic middle ground for players who want precision without the slow fire rate of a bolt-action sniper.

### Light Machine Guns (LMG)
LMGs like the M60 or the MG5 are designed for sustained suppressive fire. They typically feature large magazines and high "damage to targets out of cover," making them ideal for heavy fire support roles.

## Crafting Blueprints and Expertise

The crafting system is the backbone of long-term progression. Every time you loot a new non-exotic item, you automatically unlock its blueprint at the Base of Operations in New York. However, crafting a high-tier version of that weapon requires significant investment in your Crafting Expertise.

| Expertise Level | Green (Standard) Chance | Blue (Specialized) Chance | High-End (Gold) Chance |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Level 1** | 70% | 30% | 0% |
| **Level 5** | 20% | 60% | 5% |
| **Level 10** | 0% | 0% | **25%** |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Crafting items takes real-world time. Most high-tier blueprints require 24 hours to complete. Always keep your crafting queue full to maximize your Expertise XP gains.

As you level up your Expertise, the quality of your crafted items improves. At Expertise Level 10, you have a 75% chance of crafting a Superior item and a 25% chance of a High-End item. This makes "crafting for the sake of crafting" a viable strategy early in the game to ensure your late-game crafts are actually useful.

## Weapon Proficiency and Tactical Augmentations

Beyond basic crafting, you can further enhance specific **division resurgence weapons** through the Proficiency and Augmentation systems. This allows you to "specialize" in a favorite firearm, making it significantly more powerful than a base version of the same gun.

1.  **Proficiency Levels:** By using a specific weapon (e.g., a Vector) to neutralize enemies that are within 5 levels of your character, you earn Proficiency points. Reaching Level 10 Proficiency grants permanent stat boosts like +5% Accuracy or improved Vertical Stability.
2.  **Tactical Augmentations:** These are rare resources found in the open world. Investing them into a blueprint provides massive percentage-based buffs. For example, a fully augmented Vector blueprint can grant up to 80% additional damage.
3.  **Power Tier Enhancements:** Once you reach level 30, you can upgrade Tier 0 blueprints to Tier 1, providing a substantial jump in base stats and power score.

## Recalibration and Equipment Improvement

Once you reach Level 20, you unlock the Equipment Improvement Station. This is where you fine-tune your High-End and Exotic gear to create "God Rolls." 

Recalibration allows you to swap out a single undesirable attribute or talent for a different one. For instance, if your sniper rifle has "Received Healing" but you want "Weapon Piercing Damage," you can roll for a new stat. 

**The Rules of Recalibration:**
*   **One Slot Only:** Once you recalibrate a specific attribute or talent, all other slots on that item are locked and cannot be changed.
*   **Repeatable Rolls:** You can re-roll the *same* slot as many times as you want, provided you have the resources.
*   **High-End Only:** Recalibration is reserved for High-End (Gold) and Exotic (Red) items. Superior (Purple) gear cannot be recalibrated.

## Summary of Weapon Progression

To maximize your efficiency in the field, follow this progression loop:
*   **Early Game:** Craft anything and everything to hit Expertise Level 10 as quickly as possible.
*   **Mid Game:** Focus on using 1-2 specific weapon types to grind Proficiency levels.
*   **End Game:** Farm High-End drops, then use the Recalibration station to swap out the weakest stat for a perfect attribute roll.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I unlock blueprints for specific Division Resurgence weapons?**

A: Blueprints are unlocked automatically when you find the corresponding weapon as loot or receive it as a mission reward. Note that Exotic weapons do not have blueprints and must be found as whole items.

**Q: Can I upgrade the level of my current weapon?**

A: Yes. You can use the "Improve" function in the build menu to raise a weapon's level to match your current character level. This costs basic resources and should be done frequently to maintain your power score.

**Q: What is the benefit of Tactical Augmentations?**

A: Tactical Augmentations are rare investments into a specific weapon blueprint. They provide massive, permanent damage bonuses (up to 80%) for every future version of that weapon you craft.

**Q: Why is my crafting taking 24 hours?**

A: This is a standard mechanic in Division Resurgence. High-tier items have a lengthy crafting timer. It is recommended to start your crafts at the end of your play session so they are ready the following day.
